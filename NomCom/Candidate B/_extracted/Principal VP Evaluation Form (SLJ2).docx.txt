[Table 1 Row 1 Col 2]
Sarah has been in environmental consulting her entire career. She joined Barr’s Minneapolis office in 2009 with the AR BU working solely for Enbridge. In 2016, she relocated to Duluth and joined the EM BU while expanding her client base beyond Enbridge. Sarah’s propensity to build new, trusting relationships across a diverse set of internal and external clients, coupled with her leadership skills, have resulted in her being a highly sought-after generalist with unique skillsets in building multidisciplinary teams specializing in environmental review and permitting.
Sarah is an action-oriented project manager who excels at delivering on schedule-driven, challenging projects. Since returning to Barr after a short hiatus in 2020-21, Sarah’s focus has been on decarbonization projects, which includes developing relationships with power sector clients for renewable energy and transmission lines and assisting in Barr’s organization and delivery of newer services required as part of the decarbonization movement (e.g., solar on reclaimed coal mines and Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG)). Success in these areas has been fueled by market conditions and her desire to contribute to the growth of the environmental review (ER) practice. In addition to her focus on the power sector, Sarah built her career at Barr working across industries and for a variety of project types and clients.
Sarah is known for her persistence and her ability to get things done and to think strategically. As a result, her colleagues seek her out as a coach, and she is often asked to assist peers with establishing a project’s environmental review strategy. She is also asked to contribute to internal initiatives and lead internal teams. Sarah has served as the Environmental Review Practice Group (PG) Coordinator and EM’s Planning coordinator. She led the strategic planning teams for Improving PG Effectiveness (2018), the ESG and Sustainability Significant Strategic Investment (SSI) (2022-2023), and Phase 5 of EM’s acquisition process for Ecosphere (2023), among others. In 2024, she participated in the structural evolution team assessing the business unit structure, focused on PG structure.

[Table 2 Row 10 Col 2]
Sarah embraces Barr’s core values of collaborative consulting and caring about her colleagues. She consistently receives feedback about being a good team player, and goes out of her way to help develop others at Barr. Colleagues have noted their appreciation for Sarah’s willingness to do the little things like documenting “exceptional contributions” to keep the team motivated, as well as her help in talking through permitting strategies applicability. Teammates noted they appreciate how she trusts and values them.
Sarah appropriately responds to team feedback, and makes changes based on constructive feedback received. As a project manager required to work with dozens of people in any given year, she seeks to understand what motivates her team members and acts accordingly. Given the diversity in her teams, this often involves maneuvering conflicting feedback (e.g., too little or too much direction or meetings). She also addresses inappropriate behaviors where needed and clearly outlines the implications if the feedback is ignored. The WR BU staffing coordinator once commended Sarah for sending a very direct email clarifying that a teammate would no longer be able to support the project if missed deadlines continued. This was an extreme case with unique circumstances; nonetheless, Sarah was commended by the WR BU staffing coordination for her honesty and for not avoiding the problem.
Sarah has illustrated a “one Barr” mentality by cross-selling our services (e.g., connecting our ED team to projects with Enbridge on renewables projects), encouraging others to take ownership of their careers through her coaching relationships, and helping others grow their books of business.
Sarah is a good steward of Barr’s financial resources. Her spending on internal intiatives and roles is efficient and focused. Her marketing expenditures are focused externally (e.g., creating an ESG services fact sheet, updating the GHG accounting services to account for ESG service needs, creating the mining on non-greenfields online fact sheet and project examples (which resulted in a direct project for ED), updating barr.com’s presentation of environmental review and permitting skill sets, and barr.com (project examples/blogs). Sarah also contributes to training others outside of her practice, and responds to requests for help in delivering company-wide messages like her 2024 presentation to the BU Administrative Support PG on the career advantages of maintaining diverse skill sets and taking on stretch assignments.
Rating Scale  A – excellent, strength area; B – good, some room for improvement; C – acceptable, room for growth or outside normal practice; F-unacceptable
