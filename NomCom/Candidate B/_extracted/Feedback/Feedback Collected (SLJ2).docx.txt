General Comments
Sarah has worked with Shanna throughout her time at Barr, first as a peer on several projects and then as assistant PM / PM on a project after Sarah expressed an interest in learning more about the environmental review (ER) process. Since Shanna became a PIC, she has relied on Sarah as an experienced PM, particularly on large projects.
Sarah is one of a few Barr PMs who can take on challenging problems and solve them. Shanna is aware of criticism that Sarah can overthink things but, on MN EIS transmission line projects, it has been a real strength (e.g., when processing hundreds of public comments and distilling them into what is important to move a project forward). However, on the project where Sarah was the assistant PM, she initially developed a very robust engagement plan when a higher-level, more succinct plan was more appropriate. This type of misunderstanding has been addressed as their relationship has deepened and the appropriate level of effort is discussed and clearly established up front with both Sarah and Shanna providing input.
Sarah’s tendency to dig deep into the weeds on projects is an asset when providing direction to her team members. She provides excellent guidance (including details and reference documents) when delegating. It is also an asset when Barr is asked open-ended, unique, “what if” questions that require some digging and thinking “outside of the box”. Sarah is one of the few people in the environmental review practice who can take a nebulous concept and put together an action plan to address the need.
Sarah thinks strategically about possible opportunities for Barr.
Regarding developing long-term client relationships, Shanna noted that ER projects are often one-off and the client may not need further assistance for several years. Consequently, many ER projects result from existing long-term relationships with others at Barr (internal clients are just as important as external clients for the environmental review practice).
Regarding the criticism about Sarah “pursuing shiny objects”, Sarah may benefit by focusing on a particular industry or a particular service that is applicable across multiple industries.
On large projects, Sarah may need more experience to understand the level of effort to avoid underestimating the project cost.
Sarah is actively growing her project team. She is developing Tyler Conley as an assistant PM (engaging him in client meetings) and Veronica Parsell. After hiring several entry level staff, the ER PG has identified the need to hire an ER PM. Sarah is getting to know and engage with folks in the Four Corners area.
Shanna is not aware of any potential fatal flaws or derailers to Sarah’s candidacy.
Strengths
Sarah is a super strong PM, very organized.
Sarah is really good at working with team members that other PMs may find a bit challenging, showing tenacity in helping these individuals be successful.
Sarah is willing to try a lot of things that others won’t.
Development Needs
Sarah may benefit from balancing the courage to take on new subject areas with the risks of doing so. However, Sarah is actively working on this by taking on PM roles for two large transmission line environmental review projects, which has helped her translate her permitting skill set into the newer (to her) environmental review space.
