Joel Trinkle | Joel Trinkle | Joel has worked closely with Beau as PIC on Cerilon and HF Sinclair projects over the last two years | Joel has worked closely with Beau as PIC on Cerilon and HF Sinclair projects over the last two years | Paul Taylor (8/13/2024)
General Comments Joel has seen phenomenal development by Beau over the last two years. The Cerilon gas to liquid (GTL) permitting project in ND was Beau’s first PSD project and he was instrumental in managing many Barr SMEs from multiple BUs to prepare a large Certificate of Site Compatibility (CSC) application. Although Amanda Gravseth was the designated PM, Beau billed twice the number of hours and was effectively the PM. Beau prepared client staff for engagement with public hearings and the ND Public Service Commission. During this project, Joel identified that Beau struggles with adapting his communication style to different audiences. Beau demonstrated a marked improvement in this over the course of the project, as he built trusting relationships with project participants from diverse perspectives (e.g., owner, project manager, environmental staff, process engineers, etc.). To help develop Beau’s alternative communication styles further, Joel also encouraged Beau to take on preparing and delivering Advanced Refinery NSPS/MACT and Advanced Tanks training for HF Sinclair. Beau rose to the challenge and the 3.5-day training will take place in Tulsa OK in September. Joel and Beau will tag-team the facilitation of these classes and will hold a post-mortem with the client on any opportunities to improve, as we expect that Barr will deliver this training every other year. Beau proactively suggested to Monroe Energy (one of his longer-term clients) that Barr provide training to them on air permitting. Monroe agreed and Beau is essentially acting as a PIC by engaging Joel and Dane to perform the training. In his role as the EMBU Technology Coordinator role, Beau has become more engaged over time. He has worked well with Brian Fitch and Kyong Song on using AI to help prepare proposals and is working to develop technical solutions to integrate the network drives, SharePoint and OneDrive. Beau is also taking on a significant new challenge in preparing a CEMS manual for HF Sinclair. Beau has a good number of EM team member supporting him (e.g., folks in Duluth including Dane Jensen, Sam Anthonijs, Amanda Gravseth), in addition to many staff outside of EM. Joel is not aware of any potential de-railers for Beau’s candidacy. Strengths Beau has been great to work with and has been willing to take on challenges beyond his existing knowledge base. He has developed strong relationships with clients and many Barr staff across multiple disciplines. Development Needs Beau needs to gain additional experience in decision-making on complex issues, specifically where stakeholders are coming from different perspectives and have competing needs. Beau could become better at reviewing/editing deliverables. Joel had to help ensure the Cerilon CSC application and CEMS manual (which were written both by Beau and many others) were internally consistent and flowed well. Beau was open to learning from Joel’s comments/edits. Beau is good about providing sufficient time for Joel and others to perform review and QA on work products.
