I. Background Information
Principal vice presidents are officers of Barr Engineering Co. who lead execution of, and maintain the client relationship during, project assignments. The Officer Charter states that Barr relies on officers to behave ethically; employ good judgment in balancing company, staff, and client concerns; and model a willingness to put the long-term success of Barr Engineering Co. first. All officers are responsible for risk management, safety, professional ethics, maintaining a respectful workplace, staff development, and participation in company leadership.
In addition to the general rights, responsibilities, and expectations for a company officer, principal vice presidents have additional specific responsibilities related to projects, business development and client relationship management, and staff development as described in the Principal Vice President Role Description. The specific evaluation criteria for determining if an individual is ready to become a principal vice president are summarized in the Officer Charter.
The purpose of this Principal Development Plan (PrDP) template is to provide a tool for principal candidates (and their coaches) to:
objectively assess the candidate’s strengths, weaknesses and experience gaps relative to the Offier Charter, Principal Vice President Role Description, and the Criteria for New Principals and Officers
document a plan to build upon their strengths, mitigate their weaknesses, and fill gaps as identified during the assessments
document short term and longer term goals to complete the plan
track progress

VI. Your short-term development objectives, action plan, and continued education
Identify one or two development objectives for the coming year, and how those objectives will support your internal and external clients. For each objective, identify specific actions you will take to accomplish that result.
