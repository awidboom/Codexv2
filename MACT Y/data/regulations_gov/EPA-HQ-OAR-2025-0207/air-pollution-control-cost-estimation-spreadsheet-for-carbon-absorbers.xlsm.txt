This spreadsheet allows users to estimate the capital and annualized costs for installing and operating carbon adsorbers to control emissions of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and hazardous air pollutants (HAP) from industrial waste gas streams. Carbon adsorbers are a non-destructive control technology employed to remove volatile organic compounds (VOCs) from low to medium concentration gas streams. Adsorption is generally used when a stringent outlet concentration must be met and/or when recovery of the VOC is desired. Carbon adsorbers may be operated in either intermittent or continuous modes. The carbon is regenerated by desorbing the VOC from the carbon. This can be done off-site or onsite. The basic equipment required for a carbon adsorber system includes one or more carbon vessels or canisters and auxiliary equipment, such as duct work, pumps and storage vessels to collect VOC desorbed from the activated carbon. The desorbed VOC can be reused onsite, sold to a recycler or disposed.
The calculation methodologies used in this spreadsheet are those presented in the U.S. EPA's Air Pollution Control Cost Manual.  This spreadsheet is intended to be used in combination with the Control Cost Manual. For a detailed description of the technology and cost methodology, see Section 3.1 (VOC Recapture Controls), Chapter 1 (Carbon Adsorbers) of the Air Pollution Control Cost Manual (as updated in 2018). Additional control technologies may be necessary for some industrial waste gas streams.  Costs for additional control technologies can be estimated using the methods provided in other chapters of the cost manual.  A copy of the Control Cost Manual is available on the U.S. EPA's "Technology Transfer Network" website at: https://www.epa.gov/economic-and-cost-analysis-air-pollution-regulations/cost-reports-and-guidance-air-pollution.
The spreadsheet can be used to estimate capital and annualized costs for three types of carbon adsorbers: fixed-bed carbon adsorbers with steam regeneration; large carbon canisters with carbon replacement, and small carbon canisters with canister replacement. The methodology is designed to provide study-level (+-30%) cost estimates.  Actual costs, particularly those for installation, can deviate significantly from the costs generated using this spreadsheet due to site conditions. Units of measures in this spreadsheet are English units.
Instructions
Step 1: Please select the Data Inputs tab and click on the Reset Form button. This will reset the values on the Data Inputs tab.
Step 2:  Use the drop down menus on the Data Inputs tab to select the type of carbon adsorber system (i.e., fixed-bed or canister). For fixed-bed adsorbers, use the drop down menus to enter the operating method (i.e., continuous or intermittent), material used for adsorber vessel fabrication and to specify whether the vessels are horizontal or vertical.
Step 3:  Complete all of the yellow data entry cells on the Data Inputs tab. Many of the fields are pre-populated with default values.  Where possible, replace default values provided with site-specific values in accordance with the recommendations provided in the Control Cost Manual. Use of actual values other than the default values in this spreadsheet is recommended. Sources for all values used should be appropriately documented.
Step 4: Once all of the data fields are complete, select the Design Parameters tab to see the calculated design parameters for your condenser. Select the Cost Estimate tab to view the estimated installation and operation costs for the carbon adsorber.
