Moreover, the Toxics NSR program targets the specific location at which the source will be causing the emissions and requires an analysis of impacts on specific sensitive receptors that may be located near that source. In this way, the Toxics NSR program is a better tool to address the potential for health risks from specific sources on nearby receptors, compared with the BACT requirement under Regulation 2, Rule 2, which uses a one-size-fits-all applicability threshold that applies to all sources regardless of whether they will be located near any sensitive receptors or not. For all of these reasons, the District’s Toxics NSR program is the best tool to address potential health risks from PM2.5 sources (in addition to other tools outside of the District’s regulations, such as CEQA).
