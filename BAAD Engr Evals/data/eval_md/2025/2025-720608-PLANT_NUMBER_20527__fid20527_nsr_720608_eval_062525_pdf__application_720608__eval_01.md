The Diesel engine will be used as a backup power source for the Oakland Unified School District, 
helping to  mitigate emergency power failure s. The engine will be able to operate unrestricted during 
emergency use events. The engine will be limited to a maximum of 50 hours per year for 
maintenance and testing. The criteria pollutants associated with the source are nitrogen oxides 
(NO x), carbon monoxide (CO), precursor organic compounds (POC), sulfur dioxide (SO 2), and 
particulate matter (PM 10 and PM 2.5). 
 
S-2 meets  the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources Board (EPA/CARB) 
Tier 3 Off-road standard. The engine  will burn commercially available California low sulfur diesel 
fuel.  The sulfur content of the diesel fuel will not exceed 0.0015% by weight.   
  
Oakland Unified School District  initially submitted  this application on August 15 , 2024. Therefore, 
the current imposed BACT standard  (12/02/2024)  for criteria pollutant emission factors for IC 
Engine -compression ignition stationary emergency  diesel engine  equal or larger 50 bhp and smaller 
than 1000 bhp does not apply to this application.
