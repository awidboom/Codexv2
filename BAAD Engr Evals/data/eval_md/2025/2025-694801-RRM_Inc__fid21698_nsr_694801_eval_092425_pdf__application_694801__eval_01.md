Background
nd  
RRM , Inc. has requested a Change of Condition s to an existing Permit to Operate  for the equipment below:  
 
S-1 Portable Soil Vapor Extraction (SVE) System  
Make: Deker Technologies Inc., Model: VMX0153K Blower  
Maximum Outflow: 250 SCFM, Abated by: A -1 
 
A-1       SVE Abatement System consisting of Electric Catalytic Oxidizer or  
             Granulated Activated Carbon (GAC) Vessels , 2-200-pound vapor -phase GAC units in series  
 
The existing portable Soil Vapor Extraction (SVE) system (S -1) is intended to be operated at 1319 Howard 
Avenue, Burlingame, CA 94010, which is within 1,000 feet of two K -12 schools.  This is not within an 
Overburdened Community (OBC) as defined in Air Dis trict Regulation 2 -1.                    
 
The source, S -1, will be used to remediate soil and groundwater with chlorinated Volatile Organic 
Compounds (VOC) from the former Caine Cleaners site. The equipment consists of a vacuum blower with 
a maximum capacity of 250 SCFM, however, this capacity will be limited to 250 SCFM as agreed upon by 
the applicant. Extracted soil vapors will be directed to granulated activated carbon adsorption vessels in 
series (A -1) with 90% abatement efficiency for treatment prior to disch arge to the atmosphere.  
 
In addition, RRM, Inc. is permitted to operate an electric catalytic oxidizer as part of the portable system; 
however, according to the application data, the applicant will operate at this location using exclusively 
activated carbon vessels as an abatement  device.  
 
The main compound found in the soil was perchloroethylene (PCE); other compounds were also detected 
and will be included in the emissions calculations. Furthermore, methylene chloride, an undetected 
compound, will be added to the emissions since it is a to xic air contaminant (TAC) commonly found in 
former dry cleaners’ sites. Therefore, pursuant to the BAAQMD TAC Emission Factor Guidelines,  half of 
the detection limit will be used to calculate emissions.  
 
A startup test will be required to verify that no toxic air contaminants (TAC) emissions exceed the acute 
and chronic limits outlined in Regulation 2 -5, Table 1.  
 
Procedures are outlined in the conditions found below. The applicant will be required to provide written 
notification at the start of the operation.  The applicant will be required to stay below the acute and chronic 
trigger levels of Regulation 2 -5. Efflu ent volatile organic compound (VOC) concentrations will be 
monitored with a flame -ionization detector (FID).

## BACT

Best Available Control Technology (BACT)
T)  
In accordance with Regulation 2 -2-301, Best Available Control Technology (BACT) is triggered for any 
new or modified source with the potential to emit 10 pounds or more per highest day of POC, NPOC, 
nitrogen oxides (NO x), carbon monoxide (CO), sulfur dioxides (SO 2), particulate matter less than 10 
micrometer (PM 10) and particulate matter less than 2.5 micrometer (PM 2.5). 
 
NPOC and POC emissions are expected to be below 10 lb/day for S -1.  Therefore, BACT is not required.

 Offsets
Pursuant to Regulation 2 -2-302, offsets must be provided for any new or modified source at a facility that 
emits, or is permitted to emit, more than 10 tons per year of precursor organic compounds (POCs) or 
nitrogen oxides (NO X).  Furthermore, pursuant to Regulation 2 -2-303 offsets must be provided for any new 
or modified source at a major facility with a cumulative increase that exceeds 1.0 ton per year of PM 10, 
PM 2.5, or sulfur dioxide (SO 2).

a major facility of PM 10, PM 2.5, and SO 2.  Therefore, the requirements of Regulations 2 -2-302 and 2 -2-303 
do not apply.
