BACKGROUND
RREEF America RETI II Corp, RRR- (RREEF) has applied for an Authority to Construct for the following source: 
S-3 Emergency Standby Diesel Fire Pump Engine 
Make: John Deere, Model: OEM, Model Year: 2019 
175 bhp, 9.3 gal/hr, 1.27 MMBtu/hr 
The source will be replacing:  
S-2 Emergency Standby Diesel Fire Pump Engine 
Make: Detroit Diesel, Model: DDFP08GT4371, Model Year: 2017 
230 bhp 
The Emergency Standby Diesel Fire Pump Engine (S-3) will be located at 505 Montgomery Street in San 
Francisco, CA 94111. The engine will be used to provide power to a fire pump in case of an emergency. The 
engine for S-3 is certified to meet the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources Board 
(EPA/CARB) Tier 3 standard. The engine will burn commercially available California low sulfur diesel fuel. The 
sulfur content of the diesel fuel will not exceed 0.0015% by weight. This engine will be replacing S-2, Emergency 
Standby Diesel Fire Pump engine.

Offset Requirements, POC and NO x (Section 2-2-302) 
This section establishes emission offset requirements for POC and NO x at facilities that will have the potential to 
emit more than 10 tons per year of POC or NO x. If the facility will have the potential to emit more than 10 tons 
per year but less than 35 tons per year of NO x or POC after the new or modified source is constructed, offsets must 
be provided at a 1:1 ratio for any un-offset cumulative increase in emissions at the facility. These offsets shall be 
provided by the District’s Small Facility Banking Account unless the applicant owns offsets. If the facility emits 
more than 10 tons of POC and/or NO x per year, offsets must be provided. Based on potential to emit, the POC and 
NOx emissions will not exceed 10 tpy. Therefore, the engine is not subject to the offset requirements of Regulation 
2-2-302. 
Offset Requirement, PM 2.5, PM10 and SO x (2-2-303) 
Since the potential to emit PM 2.5, PM10 or SO x at the facility where this engine operates are each below 100 tons 
per year, this engine is not subject to the offset requirements of Regulation 2-2-303 .
