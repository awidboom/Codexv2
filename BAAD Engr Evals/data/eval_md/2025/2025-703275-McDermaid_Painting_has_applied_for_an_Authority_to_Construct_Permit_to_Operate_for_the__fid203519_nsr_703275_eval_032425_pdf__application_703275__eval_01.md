BACKGROUND
McDermaid Painting  has applied for an Authority to Construct/Permit to Operate for the 
following equipment:  
 
S-2 Paint Spray Booth  
Wood  Products Coating Operation with Exhaust Filters  
 
S-2 is an existing spray booth that was operated by the previous owner of the facility 
(previously  Yeaman Autobody  Facility# 2628). McDermaid Painting wishe s to authorize 
operation of the spray booth with this application. As defined in Regulation 2 -1-232.2, S -
2 is a new source because it has not been in operation for more than a year and has not held 
a valid permit to operate during this period of non -operation.   
 
McDermaid will manually paint residential wood products such as cabinets and doors using 
an airless spray gun inside of S -2. S-2 measures approximately 14 feet wide by 25 feet long 
and 9 feet tall. McDermaid expects to operate  S-2 for 8 hours per day and 260 days per 
year.

POC emissions are estimated using a material balance and Particulate matter with 
aerodynamic diameter smaller than or equal to a nominal 10 microns (PM 10) and Particulate 
matter with aerodynamic diameter smaller than or equal to a nominal 2.5 microns (PM2.5)emissions are estimated by materials used as follows:
POC  = Usage (gal/day or year) × POC content (lb/gal)  
PM 10 = Usage (gal/day or year) × Density (lb/gal)  × Solids % (wt %) × (1 -TE) × (1 -FE) 
PM 2.5 = Usage (gal/day or year) × Density (lb/gal)  × Solids % (wt %) × (1 -TE) × (1 -FE) 
 
Where,  
 
 2 TE = Transfer Efficiency of the airless spray gun, and  
FE = Filter Efficiency of the spray booth exhaust filters  
 
The transfer efficiency for airless spray is presumed to be 65%1 and the filtration efficiency 
for the exhaust  filters on the spray booth is presumed to  be 90% per Chapter 5.1, Spray 
Booths & Spray Guns,  of the Air District’s Permitting Handbook .
