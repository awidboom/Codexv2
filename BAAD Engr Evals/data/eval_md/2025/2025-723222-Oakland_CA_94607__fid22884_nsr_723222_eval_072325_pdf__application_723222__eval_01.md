BACKGROUND
1111 Broadway has applied for an Authority to Construct/Permit to Operate for the 
following equipment: 
S-9 Diesel fired Emergency Fire Pump engine 
Engine Make: Cummins, Model: QSB6.7, Year: 2021, 
EPA Engine Family: MCEXL0409AAB, Serial number: 74863308, 
295 bhp; 1.69 MMBtu/hour (12.3 gallons/hour); Tier 3  
 
Permit Condition Nos. 100072 and 100073 
 
The source replaces: 
 
S-2 Diesel fired Emergency Fire Pump engine 
241 bhp, Engine Make: Caterpillar, Model: 3208T, Year: 1989 
 
This fire pump engine is located at 1111 Broadway, Oakland CA 94607 inside the parking 
garage below the building on Level C and replaces another fire pump engine, S-2 that was 
decommissioned in 2021 according to the facility’s device shutdown form received 
01/31/2025.  This application follows the issuance of a Notice of Violation, NOV #A63865  
for installing and operating equipment prior to obtaining an Authority to Construct and 
Permit to Operate in violation of Regulation 2-1-301 and 2-1-302.  The applicant has stated 
that the engine was installed on August 15, 2022. 
The criteria pollutants are nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), precursor organic 
compounds (POC) from unburned diesel fuel, sulfur dioxide (SO 2) and particulate matter 
(PM10).  All of these pollutants are briefly discussed on the Air District’s web site at 
www.baaqmd.gov . 
S-9 meets the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources Board 
(EPA/CARB) Tier 3 Off-road standard.  The engine will burn commercially available 
California low sulfur diesel fuel.  The sulfur content of the diesel fuel will not exceed 
0.0015% by weight. 
This evaluation report will discuss compliance of the proposed project with all applicable 
rules and regulations.

California Environmental Quality Act ( CEQA)
Pursuant to Regulation 2-1-311, an application for a proposed new or modified source will 
be classified as ministerial and will accordingly be exempt from the California 
Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) requirement of Regulation 2-1-310 if the Air District’s 
engineering evaluation and basis for approval or denial of the permit application for the 
project is limited to the criteria set forth in Regulation 2-1-428 and to the specific 
procedures, fixed standards, and objective measurements set forth in the Air District's 
Permit Handbook and BACT/TBACT Workbook. This project triggers Best Available 
Control Technology (BACT), therefore the permit approval is not a ministerial action. 
This project, however, is categorically exempt from CEQA as per CEQA Article 18 section 
15301 and section 15303 since the project is a new and small installation at an existing 
facility involving negligible or no expansion of use. In fact, S-9 replaces a decommissioned 
fire pump engine, S-1 with a similar heat rating of about 1.64 MMBTU/hr and will be used 
for the same purpose.

New Source Performance Standards ( NSPS) 
40 CFR 60, Subpart IIII (NSPS IIII), Standards of Performance for Stationary Compression 
Ignition Internal Combustion Engines applies to non-fire pump engines such as S-1 that are 
manufactured after April 1, 2006.  Per §60.4205(b), S-9 is subject to the Tier 2 emissions 
standards in 40 CFR 1039, Appendix I for all pollutants. 
Applicable emission standards found in Appendix I of 40 CFR 1039 that apply to S-9 are: 
NMHC + NOx = 6.4 gram/kW-hour (4.77 gram/bhp-hour); CO = 3.5 gram/kW-hour (2.61 
gram/bhp-hour); and PM = 0.20 gram/kW-hour (0.15 gram/bhp-hour). 
The opacity standards in 40 CFR 89.113 it appears, apply to mobile (and not stationary) 
non-road CI engines.  Therefore, S-9 is not subject to the above standards.  Instead, S-9 is 
subject to the opacity standards in Regulation 6, Rule 1, which were discussed above. 
Per §60.4207(b), S-9 is subject to the following diesel fuel requirements in 40 CFR 
80.510(c): 
 Sulfur content ≤ 15 ppm 
 Minimum Cetane index = 40 or maximum aromatic content of 35% by volume 
Diesel fuel sold in California meets the above standards. Therefore, S-9 will comply with 
the diesel fuel requirements in NSPS IIII. 
National Emissions Standards for Hazardous Air Pollutants ( NESHAP ) 
S-9 is subject to 40 CFR 63, Subpart ZZZZ, National Emission Standards for Hazardous 
Air Pollutants for Stationary Reciprocating Internal Combustion Engines because the 
engine will be constructed (~installed) on/after June 12, 2006.  Per §63.6590(c)(1), “new” 
sources such as S-9 are required to meet the requirements in Subpart ZZZZ by meeting the 
requirements in NSPS IIII.  As previously discussed, S-9 complies with NSPS IIII and 
therefore, will comply with MACT ZZZZ as well.

### General
The owner/operator is expected to comply with all applicable requirements. Key 
requirements are listed below: 
Regulation 6-1 ( Particulate Matter – General Requirements ) 
S-9 is subject to Regulation 6, Rule 1.  Opacity and visible emissions from S-9 are limited 
by Regulation 6-1-303.2 (engine used solely as a standby source of motive power) to 
Ringelmann 2. 
Regulation 6-1-305 prohibits emission of particles from any operation in sufficient number 
to cause annoyance to any other person where the particles are large enough to be visible as 
individual particles at the emission point, or of such size and nature as to be visible 
individually as incandescent particles.  S-9 is not expected to produce visible emissions or 
fallout in violation of this regulation and will be assumed to be in compliance with 
Regulation 6-1-305. 
Regulation 6-1-310 limits emissions from any source of particulate matter in excess of 343 
mg per dscm (0.15 gr. per dscf) of exhaust gas volume. S-9 is expected to comply with this 
requirement. 
S-9’s compliance with Regulation 6, Rule 1 will be confirmed by the Air District’s 
Compliance & Enforcement staff during their routine inspections. 
Regulation 9-1-301 ( Limitations on Ground Level Concentrations ) 
S-9 is subject to and is expected to comply with the applicable SO 2 limitations in Regulation 
9, Rule 1 (“Inorganic Gaseous Pollutants – Sulfur Dioxide) because the sulfur content of 
the diesel fuel is 15 ppm. 
Regulation 9-8 ( Nitrogen Oxides and Carbon Monoxide from Stationary Internal 
Combustion Engines ) 
S-9 will be operated as an emergency standby engine and therefore is not subject to the 
emission rate limits in Regulation 9, Rule 8 ("Inorganic Gaseous Pollutants – NOx and CO 
from Stationary Internal Combustion Engines").  S-9 is exempt from the requirements of 
Sections 9-8-301 through 305, 501, and 503 per Reg. 9-8-110.5 (Emergency Standby 
Engines).  S-9 is subject to and expected to comply with 9-8-330.3 (Emergency Standby 
Engines, Hours of Operation) since non-emergency hours of operation will be limited in the 
permit conditions to 34 hours per year.  S-9 is also subject to and expected to comply with 
the monitoring and record keeping requirements of Regulations 9-8-502.1 and 9-8-530, 
which are incorporated into the proposed permit conditions.
