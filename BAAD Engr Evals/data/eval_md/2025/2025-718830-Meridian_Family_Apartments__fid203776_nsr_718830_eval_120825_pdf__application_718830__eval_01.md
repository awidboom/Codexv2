Best Available Control Technology (BACT)
T)  
In accordance with Regulation 2 -2-301, BACT is triggered for any new or modified 
source with the potential to emit 10 pounds or more per highest day of POC, NPOC, 
NO x, CO, SO 2, or PM 10.  
 
As shown in Table 1, emissions of NO x exceed s 10 pounds per day and thus trigger 
BACT requirements.  
 
Per Section 2 -2-202, BACT is defined as an emission limitation, control device, or 
control technique applied at a source that is the most stringent of:  
 
➢ the most effective device or technique successfully utilized,  
➢ the most stringent emission limitation achieved by an emission control device or 
technique for the type of equipment comprising such a source,  
➢ the most effective emission control limitation for the type of equipment comprising 
such a source that is contained in an approved implementation plan of any state, or  
➢ the most effective control device or technique or most stringent emission limitation 
that is technologically feasible, taking into consideration cost -effectiveness, any 
ancillary health and environmental impacts, and energy requirements.  
 
These requirements are generally categorized as either technologically feasible and cost -
effective (termed “BACT 1”) or achieved -in-practice (termed “BACT 2”).  
 
BACT 2 is either equal to or less stringent than BACT 1. Because achieved -in-practice is 
required regardless of cost and BACT 1 is more stringent than BACT 2, an evaluation for 
what has been achieved -in-practice is first conducted.

California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA)
A)  
On May 1 4, 2019 , ROEM Corporation submitted a Notice of Preparation for the 
development of 961 -971 Meridian Avenue in San Jose for the Meridian Residential Project 
(the "Project"). As part of this Project , David J. Powers and Associates, Inc. prepared a 
Draft Environmental Impact Report ( DEIR) in January 2020  for the City of San Jose acting 
as the Lead Agency.  
 
The Project would demolish two existing single -family houses  and redevelop a 2.1-acre 
block with a new six-story, 233 -unit affordable residential building with 1,780 square feet 
of retail space and amenities including a gym, two courtyards, and a community room. The 
space would include 290 parking spaces and 63 bicycle parking spaces. The Project would 
be partially funded by Project Base d Vouchers provided by the Santa Clara County 
Housing Authority.  The DEIR found that the Project would have significant impact s on 
construction air quality, biological resources, historic resources, hazards and hazardous 
materials, and noise.  
 
As part of the DEIR, Illingworth & Rodkin, Inc. prepared the 961 -971 Meridian Avenue 
Air Quality & Greenhouse Gas Assessment (Appendix C of the DEIR). This assessment 
included operational period emissions from the Project, including vehicle trips generated 
from the site, greenhouse gas emissions from energy use, and solid waste and wastewater 
generation . The assessment indicates that the Project would not include stationary sources 
of emissions, such as generators powered by diesel engines. The assessment d etermined 
that the project would  be less than significant with mitigation measures, including basic 
dust and exhaust mitigation measures and use of Tier 3 engines for all equipment greater 
than 25 horsepower during construction.  
 
A Notice of Determination (NOD) was filed on May 1, 2020,  for the Project. The City 
Council of the City of San Jose adopted a Statement of Overriding Consideration for the 
significant an unavoidable impact to historic resources. All other significant impacts were 
mitigated to less than significant levels. The City Council adopted a r esolution certifying 
the Environmental Impact Report ( EIR) for the Meridian Apartments Project and 
approving a Special Use Permit for the Project.  
 
On December 21, 2023, Illingworth & Rodkin Inc. prepared a Backup Generator Noise 
Control Report  for ROEM Corporation addressing the City of San Jose’s Permit 
Requirements and evaluating noise impacts from the backup generator at the Project site . 
The review determined that noise controls should be installed to m itigate the impact of 
generator noise.  
 
 
 The Air District has reviewed and considered the project’s environmental impacts as 
discussed in the EIR. The Air District additionally finds that there is no new information 
of substantial importance, which was not known and could not have been known with  the 
exercise of reasonable diligence at the time the 2020  FEIR was adopted that shows that:  
 
a. The project will have one or more significant effects not discussed in the previous 
EIR;  
b. Significant effects previously examined will be substantially more severe than 
shown in the previous EIR;  
c. Mitigation measures or alternatives previously found not to be feasible would in 
fact be feasible and would substantially reduce one or more significant effects of 
the project, but the project proponents decline to adopt the mitigation measure or 
alternati ve; or  
d. Mitigation measures or alternatives which are considerably different from those 
analyzed in the previous EIR would substantially reduce one or more significant 
effects on the environment, but the project proponents decline to adopt the 
mitigation measure o r alternative.  
 
Accordingly, the Air District finds no subsequent or supplemental EIR is required for the 
modified project pursuant to CEQA section 21166 or CEQA Guidelines section 15162.
