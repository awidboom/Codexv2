BACKGROUND
Moz Designs  has requested an Authority to Construct (AC) for the following equipment :

Moz Designs  currently operates a manufacturing  operation  of prefabricated wall and ceiling panels  
at Plant #2 4785 , located at 711 Kevin Court in  Oakland , California. They currently operate three 
permitted spray booths  (S-1 to S -3) which emit POC emissions . Moz Designs wishes to install S-4 
in order to  cut design sheets of aluminum, mild steel, and stainless steel.  The laser cutter will be 
abated by  a dust collector system that is equipped with  a HEPA filter.  At S-4, metals will be  place d 
on a retractable table that i s then inserted into the enclosure of the laser cutter. Particulates are 
vented out of the bottom of the enclosure and routed to the dust collector.

Moz Design had originally applied a 5% fume generation factor to all the metal emissions they 
estimated from the laser cutter. This originate d from a  document titled “Emission of Fume, 
Nitrogen Oxide, and Noise in Plasma Cutting of Stainless and Mild Steel” by Bromsen B. et al. 
from the Swedish Institute of Production Engineering. This 1994 study posits that only 5% of metal 
is emitted via fumes, and t he rest is turned to metal slag (solids). After researching the veracity of 
this document, the Air District decided not to  use this fume generation factor, and go with a more 
conservative estimate  discussed in the calculations above .

Moz is located in an  Overburdened Community (OBC) as defined in Regulation 2 -1-243. New or 
modified sources of TACs in an OBC are held to a stringent cancer health risk of 6.0 in a million 
(vs. 10.0 in a million for non -OBC).  As proposed, Moz’s original emission control strategy wasinadequate.  Their initial dust collection system captured only 95% of the toxic particulates,
resulting in a significant  potential for exposure.  After rigorous technical review by the Air District, 
the facility was required to redesign their emissions mitigation approach and upgrade to a high -
efficiency HEPA filtration system with a 99.97% toxic particulate removal efficie ncy.  The HRA 
results are based on upgraded abatement equipment and demonstrate a significant reduction in 
potential risks.

California Environmental Quality Act ( CEQA )
This project is categorically  exempt from CEQA requirement in Regulation 2 -1-310 per CEQA 
Guidelines § 1530 1(e) and (f).  CEQA Guidelines § 15301 refers to “Existing Facilities” where 
there is no or negligible expansion of use. § 15301(e) exempts:  
“Additions to existing structures provided that the addition will not result in an increase of more 
than:  
(1) 50 percent of the floor area of the structures before the addition, or 2,500 square feet, 
whichever is less; or  
(2) 10,000 square feet if:  
(A) The project is in an area where all public services and facilities are available to allow  
for maximum development permissible in the General Plan and  
(B) The area in which the project is located is not environmentally sensitive. ” 
Both of these scenarios are met by this project , as the project does not include any expansion or 
addition of existing structures . Furthermore, abatement devices are categorically exempt due to § 
15301(f), which exempts the “addition of safety or health protection devices.”  
Moz Designs  has submitted CEQA Appendix H form i n accordance with Regulation 2 -1-426. The 
Air District has reviewed the Appendix H form and agree s that the proposed project has no 
potential for causing any adverse environmental impacts.
