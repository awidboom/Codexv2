2 The SFPUC is proposing to construct a new Water Resource Recovery Facility 
(WRRF). The new WRRF will be located at 600 Portway Passage on Treasure Island. 
The proposed facility will be replacing Plant #24945, Treasure Island Development 
Authority (TIDA), which is the existing wastewater treatment plant on Treasure Island. 
 
S-100 Wastewater Treatment System The Wastewater Treatment System (S-100) is a municipal sewage system that will treat 
incoming wastewater to tertiary standards.  
 
The following are the proposed permitted flows for the new WRRF: 
 
 Average Dry Weather Flow Rate (ADWF) – 1.3 Million Gallons per Day (MGD) 
 Peak Wet Weather Flow Rate (PWWF) – 3.9 MGD 
 
Wastewater will be treated at the WRRF as follows: 
 
S-110 Preliminary Treatment Wastewater will enter the WRRF at S-110, which is the headworks facility for the 
WRRF. Wastewater will be pumped into the facility using four (4) 1,200 gallon per 
minute (gpm) influent pumps. The pumps are driven by electric motors. The incoming 
wastewater will be treated at S-110 by: 
 
 Two (2) 5 MGD rotary drum screen/grit chambers; Model: IPEC IWCE FM 4869P 
 Two (2) 5 MGD Washer Compactors; Model: JWCE MWP0018-0090-30K-GA 
 One (1) 5 MGD Multi-tray grit separator; Model: HeadCell Grit Concentrator 
 One (1) 5 MGD Grit Classifier; Model: Hydro GritCleanse Grit Washing and 
Dewatering Unit or HUBER Coanda Grit Classifier RoSF or equivalent 
 
Grit removed from the units described above will be sent to a lined bagger and into a bin 
for off-site disposal. 
 
S-130 – Equalization Basin   The facility had initially proposed to install an equalization basin. However, the facility 
stated that the equalization basin is for future construction (more than 2 years in the 
future), and as such they will instead apply for an authority to construct for this source in 
a future permit application. 
 
S-140 – Secondary Treatment (MBR – Bioreactor Stage)   Wastewater will be treated by Membrane Bio Biological Nutrient Removal (BNR) 
comprised of four (4) bioreactor trains, comprised of a pre-anoxic basin, three (3) 
aeration basins, and a post anoxic basin. The purpose of the BNR is to provide removal 
of biological oxygen demand (BOD) and nutrients from the wastewater.

3 S-160 – Tertiary Treatment   
Following each bioreactor, the mixed liquor generated from this process will be sent into 
the MBR membrane basins. There will be four (4) membrane basin units in total. 
Suspended solids from the mixed liquor (MLSS) will be removed by filtration. There will 
be four membrane basins. It is expected that 4,000 wet lbs/day of treated activated 
sludge (TAS) will be generated per day. 
 
S-170 – Disinfection  The treated wastewater from S-160 will be sent to S-170 for disinfection. The facility will 
utilize ultraviolet (UV) disinfection at S-170. Following this stage, approximately 0.5 
MGD will be dosed with sodium hypochlorite for use as recycled water. The remaining 
treated effluent will not be dosed with sodium hypochlorite and will be discharged into a 
constructed wetland that drains to the bay. 
 
S-180 – Sludge Handling Process  Suspended solids generated by the MBR process will be handled by three (3) rotary drum thickeners. The sludge processed by this 
source will be loaded onto trucks and sent to Oceanside Treatment Plant (Plant #4116) 
for further treatment at this time. It is expected that approximately 3,800 dry lbs/day of 
TAS will be generated and shipped per day. This modification to Plant #4116 will be 
addressed in a separate application. 
 
All sources in the wastewater treatment train will either be enclosed or will be 
constructed so that foul air generated will be directed to the abatement devices. 
 
 A-1: Evoqua Biotrickling Filter, 10,600 CFM 
 A-2: Carbon Scrubbers – Two (2) Carbon Scrubber Units, 5,300 CFM each, 
running in parallel 
 
The facility states that both A-1 and A-2 will be operating at all times with the exception 
for planned maintenance, unplanned repairs, and during alternative normal operations. 
Alternative normal operations is defined as a period where only either A-1 or A-2 will 
operate. The facility has stated that during periods of alternative normal operations, the 
flow rate through the combined stack of A-1 and A-2 will be equal to that of normal 
operation, and that the facility will still be able to meet the emissions limits set forth for 
the facility. The airflow through the combined stack of A-1 and A-2 will be a minimum of 
2,160 CFM and maximum of 10,600 CFM at all times. 
 
The treated wastewater will be divided into two uses with a portion of the treated water 
being used as recycled water, and the remainder being discharged into wetlands/bay 
outfall. 
 
S-1 and S-2 - Stationary Emergency Diesel Engine Generator Sets The new WRRF will have two stationary emergency diesel engine generator sets. The 
two engine sets are identical and will provide power to the facility in the event of an 
emergency.

The WRRF sources are expected to have negligible particulate emissions and are

Ringelmann No. 2 Limitation (Section 6-1-303) 
S-1 and S-2 each have a displacement of 824 in3 and therefore are subject to the 
Ringelmann 2.0 limit in Regulation 6-1-303. The engines are expected to meet this 
requirement with regular maintenance and inspection. 
 
Visible Particles (Section 6-1-305) 
Since S-1 and S-2 will emit a very small amount of PM 10, they are not expected to 
produce visible emissions or fallout in violation of this regulation and are assumed to be 
in compliance with Regulation 6-1-305  pending a regular inspection. 
 
Total Suspended Particulate Concentration Limits (Section 6-1-310) 
The exhaust rate for this engine is 3,433 cfm. Per Regulation 6-1-310.1 , a person shall 
not emit from any source particulate matter in excess of 0.15 grains/dscf of exhaust gas 
volume. The emission rate from S-1 and S-2 is 0.052 grams/bhp-hr, which results in an 
outlet grain loading of 0.008 grains/dscf at 0% O 2. The emission rate is less than the 
limit of 0.15 grains/dscf and is in compliance with Regulation 6-1-310. 
 
The engines are not subject to Section 6-1-310.2 because they do not have the 
potential to emit more than 1,000 kg of total suspended particulate. 
 
Total Suspended Particulate (TSP) Weight Limits (Section 6-1-311) 
The engines are not subject to Section 6-1-311 because the limits are based on process 
weight as defined by Section 6-1-206, and process weight excludes liquid fuel and 
combustion air. 
 
Demonstration of Total Suspended Particles (TSP) Compliance (Section 6-1-504) 
The engines are not subject to Section 6-1-504 because they do not have the potential 
to emit more than 2,000 kg of total suspended particulate.
