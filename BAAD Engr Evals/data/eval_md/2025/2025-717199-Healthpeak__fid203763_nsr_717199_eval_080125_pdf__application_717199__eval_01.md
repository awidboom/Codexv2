West Coast Energy Systems LLC (Energy Systems)  on behalf of Healthpeak , is applying 
for an Authority to Construct/Permit to Operate for the following equipment : 
 
S-1 Emergency  Standby  Diesel Generator (Engine A)

Year: 2024, 768 bhp,  31.3 gal/hr,  4.30 MMBtu/hr

S-2 Emergency  Standby  Diesel Generator (Engine B)  
Make:  AB Volvo Penta , Model: TAD164XGE -B,   
Year: 2024, 768  bhp,  31.3 gal/hr,  4.30 MMBtu/hr  
EPA Family: RVPXL16.1ACC  
 
The criteria pollutants are nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), precursor 
organic compounds (POC) from unburned diesel fuel, sulfur dioxide (SO 2) and particulate 
matter (PM 10).  All of these pollutants are briefly discussed on the Air District’s web site 
at www.baaqmd.gov . 
 
S-1 and S -2 form part of the Generac MD1000 Gemini (MD1000GEM) equipment, which 
is a high -capacity, modular diesel generator that combines two independent 768 bhp diesel 
engine sets in a single, compact enclosure. Each engine has its own exhaust system.  
  
S-1 and S -2 meets  the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources 
Board (EPA/CARB) Tier 2 Off-road standard. The engine s will burn commercially 
available California low sulfur diesel fuel.  The sulfur content of the diesel fuel will not 
exceed 0.0015% by weight.  
 
In addition, the set includes a UL 142 Fuel Tank  to store diesel. However, the facility 
confirmed in an email correspondence on May 25 , 2025 that it is not requesting a 
Certificate of Exemption.  
 
This evaluation report will discuss compliance of the proposed project with all applicable 
rules and regulations.

technique applied at a source that is the most stringent of:   
 
 4 • the most effective device or technique successfully utilized,

technique for the type of equipment comprising such a source,

that is technologically feasible, taking into consideration cost -effectiveness, any ancillary

operation per year (50 hours) and 100 hours of emergency operation for each engine.  
 
Per Air District Regulation 9 -8-330.1 emergency operation for diesel engines is not limited, 
therefore, 100 hours is a reasonable worst -case assumption of the longest a facility may 
need to operate on backup power in any given year in the event of a major power outage. 
This presumption of 100 hours per year of emergency operation is consistent with EPA's 
approach to calculating the PTE of such generators.
