Background
ound    
Mayfair  Apartments  is applying for an Authority to Construct/Permit to Operate for the 
following equipment:    
   
S-1 Emergency -Direct Drive Diesel Fire Pump Engine   
Make: John Deere , Model: 6068HFC28A , Model Year: 2024   
64 bhp,  1.1 MMBtu/hr     
Permit Condition Nos. 100072 , 100073 and  100076  
 
The criteria pollutants are nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), precursor organic 
compounds (POC) from unburned diesel fuel, sulfur dioxide (SO 2) and particulate matter (PM 10).  
All of these pollutants are briefly discussed on the Air District’s website at www.baaqmd.gov .    
S-1 meets the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources Board 
(EPA/CARB) Tier 3  Off-road standard.  The engine will burn commercially available California 
low-sulfur diesel fuel.  The sulfur content of the diesel fuel will not exceed 0.0015% by weight.      
   
This project includes a direct drive Clarke, model JU4H -UFAEE8, that complies with National 
Fire Protection Association (NFPA) 20 (Standard for the Installation of Stationary Pumps for 
Fire Protection) and will comply with NFPA 25 (Standard for the Inspect ion, Testing, and 
Maintenance of Water -Based Fire Protection Systems), powered by a 64 bhp diesel engine.  
 
Diesel will be stored in a Pentair 119 -gallon UL 142 double wall diesel fuel tank, which may be 
eligible for the exemption of Air District Regulation 2 -1-123.1, provided a permit is not required 
per Section 2 -1-319. However, the facility confirmed via emai l on July 23, 2025, that it did not 
apply for a permit or a Certificate of Exemption for the diesel fuel tank. Therefore, the diesel fuel 
tank was not evaluated as part of this application. The applicant is aware that the burden of proof 
for demonstrating compliance with Section 2 -1-123.1 and Section 2 -1-319 rests with them per 
Section 2 -1-502. 
 
This evaluation report will discuss compliance of the proposed project with all applicable rules 
and regulations.

## CEQA
JSONPath: `$.CEQA.narrative`

California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA)
)    
The City of El Cerrito, as Lead Agency, prepared a draft supplemental EIR for the San Pablo 
Avenue Specific Plan Update, followed by a Final SEIR (October 13, 2022) that includes 
responses to comments and revisions, SCH#2014042025.   The City of El Cerrito’s CEQA 
analysis included emergency backup generators.   
 
In the Final SEIR, the Lead Agency concluded that the updated Specific Plan would not result in 
new significant air quality impacts beyond those previously evaluated, nor would it substantially 
increase the severity of previously identified significant imp acts. Construction - and operational -
phase emissions were estimated using best‐available models and methodologies, and the project 
was evaluated for consistency with applicable regional emission reduction plans and State 
climate goals. The Final SEIR includ es revisions to the greenhouse gas (GHG) and energy 
chapters, but none of those revisions altered the findings of less -than-significant impact for air 
quality or GHG.   
 
The Air District has independently reviewed and considered the Lead Agency’s environmental 
analysis, the Final SEIR, and the permit application materials submitted for individual 
development proposals under the Plan  pursuant to CEQA . Based on this review , the emergency 
backup generator of this permit application will not result in any new or substantially more 
severe impacts, so the Air District finds a subsequent/supplemental EIR is not required. Because  
the Final SEIR demonstrates that the project does not produce new or more severe significant air 
quality or GHG impacts, the Air District concludes that no additional mitigation measures are 
required under CEQA beyond those already adopted or anticipated in the SEIR. In light of that, 
CEQA does not require the Air District to evaluate alternative project designs or adopt further 
mitigation. Correspondingly, a mitigation monitoring and reporting program is not required 
under CEQA for the Air District’s acti on. 
 
Final action was taken by the Air District after conclusion of the public comment period 
associated with the Lead Agency’s CEQA certification and following full consideration of the 
Final SEIR and all supporting documentation.
