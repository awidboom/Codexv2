 The Hayward WWTP  is a municipal wastewater treatment plant th at provides primary and 
secondary treatment of wastewater from the City of Hayward . The treated effluent from The 
Hayward WWTP is combined with treated effluent from the  other members of the East Bay 
Dischargers Authority (EBDA) at the EBDA Common Outfall, where it is then discharged into 
the lower San Francisco Bay.  This project is being undergone to update aging infrastructure and 
to comply with upcoming regulations from the Regional Water Quality Control Board that will 
limit the discharge of nutrients into the Bay.  The project will include the construction of a new 
grit facility, a new biological nutrient removal (BNR) process, a new primary effluent 
equalization (PE EQ) facility, and miscellaneous improvements to existing facilities. The dry 
weather average design wastewater flow is 18.5 MGD  in the NDPES permit No. CA003 7869 
and 16.5 MGD in permit condition no. 23563 . The peak wet weather design wastewater flow is 
35.0 MGD  in the NPDES permit No. CA0037869 and 40 MGD in permit condition no. 23563 . 
The wastewater flo ws will not be changing in this project.  
 
Preliminary Treatment , S110 
Preliminary Treatment currently consists of influent screens, followed by grit and floating debris 
removal done by a vacuator process , a form of grit and scum removal . A separate grit washing 
system then divides the organics from the grit before disposal in a solid waste landfill. A new grit 
removal facility will replace the vacuator process. The existing Headworks, Site Waste Pump 
Station , and East Barminutor Pit will remain.  However, t he existing North Vacuator and grit 
washing facility will be decommissi oned following startup of the new grit removal facility.  
 
The new grit removal facility will be enclosed, with foul air routed to air dispersion fans, which 
will exhaust at P110 and will operate continuously. The air dispersion fans will elevate and move 
foul air at a flow rate of 1 ,100 scfm, but will not provide any abatement of pollutants. The 
facility has requested H 2S, ammonia , and organics  limits of 33 ppmv , 20 ppmv , and 150 ppmv 
(as methane) .    
 
Flow Equalization, S130  
The existing primary effluent (PE – effluent from the primary clarifiers) equalization basin, 
currently an open pond, will be demolished to make space for the new biological nutrient 
removal (BNR) process and replaced by the new PE EQ tanks.  The new PE EQ tanks will have 
the same capacity (3 million gallons) as the existing PE EQ basin.  Primary effluent will flow by 
gravity to either the existing trickling filter pumps, which will then feed either the existing East 
Trickling Filter or the new PE EQ tanks, o r the new PE EQ pump station.  The new PE EQ tanks 
will be used to equalize higher than normal primary effluent flows during wet weather and will 
operate intermittently. However, the PE EQ tanks will be evaluated assuming they run 
continuously to allow for operational flexibility.   
 
The new PE EQ tanks will be covered and abated by two 2,040 scfm carbon adsorption units, 
Odor Control , abating PE EQ tanks, A11, each abating one of two PE EQ tanks, for a total of 
4,080 scfm  foul air flow . The facility has requested H 2S, ammonia, and organics limits of 2.7 
ppmv, 20 ppmv, and 25 ppmv.

 The new PE EQ pump station will pump directly to the new BNR process, part of S140 , and will 
receive primary effluent by gravity from the primary clarifiers. The new PE EQ pump station 
will be an open wet well without abatement.  
 
Secondary Treatment, S140  
The existing West Trickling Filter will be demolished and replaced by the new BNR process.  
The new BNR process will consist of aeration tanks, a third final clarifier, a new blower  and 
electrical building, and other ancillary facilities. A new alkalinity addition facility will store and 
utilize sodium hydroxide to control the pH in the BNR treatment process. The BNR process will 
be constructed in the location of the existing PE EQ basin . A Mixed Liquor and Trickling Filter 
Effluent Mixing Box will be constr ucted to accommodate flow from the BNR process.  
 
The BNR process is being constructed to meet increasingly stringent standards for nutrient 
standards in effluent wastewater quality, with a specific focus on ammonia removal. This will 
result in the conversion of ammonia into N 2O but will not result in increased emissions of any 
regulated pollutant. Therefore, there will be no quantified increase of emissions at S140.  
 
Solids Contact Aeration Tank, S142  
The existing solids contact aeration tanks will be altered. They will be converted from solids 
contact aeration tanks to bioflocculation tanks, which will better integrate trickling filter effluent 
into the BNR process. This project will not result in an e missions increase at S142.  
 
Final Clarifiers ( 3), S151  
Two of the existing final clarifiers will be rehabilitated. A third final clarifier will be constructed 
as part of the improved secondary treatment process . This will not increase the wastewater flow 
rate. Wastewater entering the final clarifiers has passe d through most of the wastewater treatment 
plant process and will be very clean. Therefore, the addition of a third final clarifier will not 
result in an increase of emissions at S151.

6. OFFSETS
FSETS  
Emission offset requirements for POC and NOx are set out in Regulation 2, Rule 2, Section 302.  
POC and NOx offsets are required for new or modified sources at a facility that emits or will be 
permitted to emit 10 tons per year or more of that pollutant. This project will not result in an 
increase in NO X emissions. POC emissions will not increase to or above 10 tons per year. 
Therefore, offsets for NO X or POC are not required for this application.  
 
The offsets requirements for PM 10, PM 2.5, and SOx are specified in Regulation 2, Rule 2, Section 
303. Per Section 303, PM 10, PM 2.5, and SOx emission offsets are required for any new or 
modified source that is a major facility for PM 10, PM 2.5, or SOx emissions.  The Hayward WWTP  
is not a major facility for PM 10, PM 2.5, and SOx emissions. Therefore, offsets for PM 10, PM 2.5, 
and SOx are not required for this application.
