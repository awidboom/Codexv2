The proposed portable soil vapor extraction operation will take place at 6301 San Pablo Avenue, Oakland, 
CA 94608. 
 
The site was a gas station from approximately 1950 to 1980.  It is currently an oil changer and smog shop 
(Pro Lube).  There are no known plans to change the land use.   
 
Alameda County Environmental Health is requiring the operator of the former gas station, ExxonMobile, 
to clean up the petroleum hydrocarbons that were released during the gas station operation. Therefore, a 
soil vapor extraction operation (SVE) has been proposed for this site. 
 
 
As mentioned above, the proposed portable SVE equipment (S-1) already has a portable permit with the 
Air District. Permit Condition 27768 Part 4 currently requires a permit application to be submitted if the 
facility proposes to relocate S-1 to within 1000 feet of a K-12 school.  Under this application, the facility 
proposes to relocate S-1 to a location that will be within 1000 feet of Aspire Berkeley Maynard Academy, 
a K-12 school.   The facility is proposing a higher abatement rate of 98.0% for all pollutants for an operation 
at 6301 San Pablo Avenue, Oakland, CA 94608per correspondence on February 23, 2025. The portable 
SVE system at this site will consist of one blower with the maximum flow rate of 500 cfm. The blower will 
be abated by three 1,000-lb. granular activated carbon (GAC) vessels (3,000 lbs. total). 
 
The main compound found in the soil was benzene. Emissions calculations were based on laboratory results 
from samples taken at the site.  
 
This project is not expected to trigger Health Risk Assessment per Regulation 2-5, Table 1.  
 
Procedures are outlined in the conditions found below. The applicant will be required to provide written 
notification at the start of the operation.  The applicant will be required to stay below the acute and chronic 
trigger levels of Regulation 2-5. Effluent volatile organic compound (VOC) concentrations will be 
monitored with a flame-ionization detector (FID).  
 
There is one K-12 school within 1,000 feet of the proposed SVE system.

## BACT

Best Available Control Technology (BACT)
(BACT) 
In accordance with Regulation 2-2-301, Best Available Control Technology (BACT) is triggered for any 
new or modified source with the potential to emit 10 pounds or more per highest day of POC, NPOC, 
nitrogen oxides (NO x), carbon monoxide (CO), sulfur dioxides (SO 2), particulate matter less than 10 
micrometer (PM 10) and particulate matter less than 2.5 micrometer (PM 2.5). 
 
With the existing abatement device, NPOC and POC emissions are expected to be below 10 pound per day 
for the SVE system.  Therefore, BACT is not required.

Offsets
ffsets 
Pursuant to Regulation 2-2-302, offsets must be provided for any new or modified source at a site that 
emits, or is permitted to emit, more than 10 tons per year of precursor organic compounds (POCs) or 
nitrogen oxides (NO X).  Furthermore, pursuant to Regulation 2-2-303 offsets must be provided for any new
