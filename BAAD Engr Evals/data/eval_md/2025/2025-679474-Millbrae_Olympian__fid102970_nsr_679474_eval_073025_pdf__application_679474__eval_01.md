S-2 will operate at 1009 El Camino Real, Millbrae, CA 94030 -2013 . This is  not within  an Overburdened 
Community  (OBC) as defined in Regulation 2 -1. The site is an operating gasoline service station located 
in a mixed commercial/residential area. The installation was approved in a San Mateo County Groundwater 
Protection Program letter dated September 27,  2021 . 
 
The objective of the Multi -phase Extraction ( MPE ) system is to remove residual hydrocarbons in the 
subsurface soil. MPE is a  technology that extracts soil vapor through a high vacuum applied to the 
subsurface.  The MPE system includes a 85-cfm, 20 hp vacuum blower and emissions will be abated by a 
thermal/catalytic oxidizer (A -1). Soil vapor will be  extracted from up to eight site wells by applying vacuum 
to the well casings. After extraction from the wells,  the soil vapor will pass through a vapor/liquid se parator, 
where any moisture will be separated out and soil  vapor will be routed to A-1 for abatement.  
 
The catalytic oxidation system is capable of destroying 9 8.5% of  VOCs . A-1 uses natural gas to operate 
and secondary emissions are expected .  
 
The soil data used to calculate the VOC emissions is from the  analytical report prepared by  McCampbell  
Analytical, Inc. in January  2012.  
 
Procedures are outlined in the conditions found below.  The applicant will be required to provide written 
notification at the start of the operation.  The applicant will be required to stay below the acute and chronic 
trigger levels of Regulation 2 -5.
