 This site is a former dry cleaner.  San Francisco Bay Regional Water Quality Control Board first 
identified this site  as a potential contributor to soil vapor issues that led to the closure of  
McClymonds High School  in 2020. Consequential investigation found  elevated levels of 
perchloroeth ylene (PCE) related to former dry cleaner operations at the site.   
 
Based on the 5 -day pilot test, the facility was successful in abating 99.9997% of the PCE 
concentrations . Subsequent monitoring results  showed that high levels of PCE were still present  
in the subsurface layer .  
 
The facility is proposing to conduct a soil vapor extraction (SVE) operation in order to mitigate 
potential health risk s on site.  
 
The SVE  system at this site will consist of one blower  with the maximum flow rate of 150 cfm. A 
flow meter will be installed to ensure that the flow rate is not exceeded. The blower will be abated 
by three 300 -lb. to 400 -lb. permanganate -infused granular activated carbon (GAC) vessels (total 
1,200 lbs. to 1,600 lbs.)  in series .

A-2 
 Portable vapor analyzers respond to different compounds with differing levels of sensitivity.  
To calibrate each instrument, a standard calibration gas is used (methane for FIDs, isobutylene for 
PIDs).  
 
To determine an instrument reading for a compound other than the one used in the calibration gas, 
the reading must be adjusted using a response factor that is specific to the compound of interest 
and the concentration range of interest.  
 
Response Factor Multiplier = (Actual Concentration) / (Measured Response)  
 
This equation may be adjusted to determine the actual concentration of a compound from an 
instrument measured response (instrument reading) and the instrument’s response factor 
multiplier for that compound.  
 
Actual Concentration = (Response Factor Multiplier) x (Measured Response)  
  
The portable vapor analyzers models that are frequently used include the PID MiniRae 2000, 
PID TVA -1000B, FID TVA -1000B, and FID TVA -2020.  
 
For the low concentration range (less than 10 ppm), the PID MiniRae (calibrated with 
isobutylene) has a response factor multiplier for tetrachloroethylene of either 0.69 (if using the 
9.8 eV lamp), 0.57 (if using the 10.6 eV lamp), or 0.31 (if using the 11 .7 eV lamp)1. The PID 
TVA -1000B has a multiplier of 0.738 and the FID TVA -1000B has a multiplier of 0.9742. A 
response factor for tetrachloroethylene has not been published for the FID TVA -2020.  
 
Due to the wider range of organic compounds that may be detected with an FID and the more 
approximate response of an FID to tetrachloroethylene, in particular, an FID will be required to 
be used for ongoing compliance demonstrations.
