generator located outdoors near the Eastern border of the property  next to two other  engine  
generator s (S-32 and S -33) permitted under AN 32207 and 703302.  
 
The applicant states the initial date of operation of S -38 was 9/26/2024.  
 
S-38 meets the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources Board 
(EPA/CARB) Tier 4 Off -road standard. The engine will burn commercially available 
California low sulfur diesel fuel. The sulfur content of the diesel fuel will not exceed 
0.0015% by wei ght. The criteria pollutants associated with this engine’s operation are 
nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), precursor organic compounds (POC) from 
unburned diesel fuel, sulfur dioxide (SO 2) and particulate matter (PM 10). All these 
pollutants are briefly discussed on the District’s web site at www.baaqmd.gov. 
 
Portable engines are non -road engines as defined by 40 CFR 1039.801 and 40 CFR 1068.30  
of the Federal Clean Air Act. Appendix A to Subpart A of 40 CFR 1074 does not allow 
states “or political subdivisions” to impose emission control on non- road engines. The 
exception is standards and other requirements imposed by the State of California necessary 
to achieve attainment of air pollution standards.   The regulatory a nalysis for this 
application will take this into account.
