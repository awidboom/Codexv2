with integral Selective Catalytic  Reduction and Diesel Particulate Filter  
 
S-5 Emergency Standby Diesel Generator Set  
Make: Caterpillar Inc, Model: 2806F -E18TAG1 , Model Year: 2025 
744 bhp, 5.019  MMBtu/hr     
Permit Condition Nos. 100072 and 100073, and 27778  
with integral Selective Catalytic  Reduction and Diesel Particulate Filter  
  
The S -3, S-4, and S- 5 engines are within one enclosure. The criteria pollutants are nitrogen 
oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), precursor organic compounds (POC) from unburned 
diesel fuel, sulfur dioxide (SO 2) and particulate matter (PM 10).  All of these pollutants are briefly 
discussed on the Air District’s website at www.baaqmd.gov.    
 
S-3, S-4, and S- 5 meet the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources Board 
(EPA/CARB) Tier 4 Off-road standard.  The engine will burn commercially available California 
low-sulfur diesel fuel.  The sulfur content of the diesel fuel will not exceed 0.0015% by weight.     
   
This evaluation report will discuss compliance of the proposed project with all applicable rules 
and regulations.

Health Risk Assessment (HRA)
(HRA)    
The diesel particulate emissions from the project are greater than the toxic trigger level of 0.26 
lb/year. All PM 10 emissions are considered diesel particulate emissions. The PM 10 emissions from 
this application are summarized in Table 1. There is one related project ( S-2 under AN 636652)  
completed in the past five years.   Since the diesel particulate emissions from the project are 
greater than the toxic trigger level of 0.26 lb/year, an HRA is required.   
 
This application did not qualify for HRA streamlining because receptors are located less than 
100 feet from the proposed engine location. The project is in compliance with project risk 
requirements as recommended, limiting reliability -related activity hours by permit condition. See 
HRA report.
