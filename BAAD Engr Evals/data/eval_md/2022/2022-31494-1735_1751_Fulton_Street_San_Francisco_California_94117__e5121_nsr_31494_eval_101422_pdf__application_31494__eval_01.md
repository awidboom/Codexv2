The site consists of two buildings located at 1735 and 1751 Fulton Street in San Francisco.  The eastern 
building (1735 Fulton) was previously operated as a dry cleaner.  The western building (1751 Fulton) was 
previously occupied by a restaurant and bar and is currently unoccupied.  Historical operations at 1735 
Fulton released tetrachloroethene (PCE) into the soil beneath both buildings.  Lab analysis of the soil 
beneath the buildings shows the presence of a variety of chlorinated and non-chlorinated volatile organic 
compounds (VOC), with PCE as the primary contaminant of concern.  Farallon Consulting has proposed 
to install S-1, a soil vapor extraction system (SVE), to remediate the contaminated soil. 
 
S-1 will include a maximum 418-cfm vacuum blower to remove VOCs.  Contaminated soil vapor will be 
routed through a series of three (3) 1,000-lb activated carbon vessels to remove VOCs before reaching the 
atmosphere.  Emissions are based on laboratory results of soil vapor submitted in this application for the 
site.  S-1 will be located within 1,000 ft of a K-12 school and is subject to a public notice. 
 
Procedures are outlined in the conditions found below.  Effluent volatile organic compound (VOC) 
concentrations will be monitored with a flame-ionization detector (FID) on a schedule reflecting current 
loading rates and predicted carbon capacity.  Monitoring schedule changes will be allowed based on 
monitoring data collected.

Pursuant to Regulation 2-5-301, a new or modified source is subject to TBACT if the source cancer risk 
exceeds 1.0 in a million and/or if the chronic hazard index exceeds 0.20.   
 
The estimated cancer risk for S-1 exceeds 1.0 in a million and therefore the source is subject to TBACT for 
emissions of POC and NPOC.  Section 9 of the Air District’s BACT/TBACT Workbook has guidelines for 
Soil Vapor Extraction operations. If technologically feasible and cost-effective, TBACT 1 for POC requires 
the effluent volumetric concentration of POC to be less than or equal to 10 ppmv; or POC must be abated 
with a capture/destruction efficiency of at least 98.5%.  The typical technology to achieve this requirement 
is by at least two (2) activated carbon vessels in series or a thermal oxidizer.  TBACT 1 for NPOC requires 
the effluent volumetric concentration of NPOC to be less than or equal to 10 ppmv.  The typical technology 
to achieve this requirement is at least two (2) activated carbon vessels in series.  Although the TBACT 
requirement for NPOC allows for an effluent volumetric concentration up to 10 ppmv, the effluent NPOC 
volumetric concentration for this source would not be allowed exceed 6.05 ppmv to ensure that the project 
risk does not increase.

carbon vessels in series.  Furthermore, the applicant has agreed to the emission control requirements for 
POC and NPOC.  With the maximum effluent volumetric concentration of NPOC not to exceed 6.05 ppmv.
