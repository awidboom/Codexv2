Kie-Con has proposed to install a stationary dual compartment cement silo (S -23), abated by two 
baghouses (A-23, and A-24) at 3551 Wilbur Avenue, Antioch, California. Kie -Con manufacturers large 
precast concrete items such as pilings, girders, and wall panels used in construction. The cement silo will 
be used to allow additional storage space of cement and cement supplement, to alleviate issues related to 
supply chain interruptions .

Regulation 2, Rule 5: Permits – New Source Review of Toxic Air Contaminants
The District’s regulation for toxic air contaminant (TAC) emissions is codified in Regulation 2 -5, New 
Source Review of Toxic Air Contaminants.  Pursuant to Regulation 2 -5-110, a project shall not be subject 
to this rule if, for each TAC, the total project emissions are below the acute and chronic triggers levels 
listed in Table 2 -5-1 of this regulation. A project includes all related  new or modif ied sources of TACs 
within a 5 -year period. Kie-Con does not have any related project within the 5 -year lookback period . 
Therefore , project emissions of TACs will only include the emissions summarized in Table  5. 
 
Based on the trigger levels listed  in Regulation 2 -5, Table 2 -5-1, the proposed TAC emissions from the  
project  exceed the acute and chronic  trigger level s for arsenic , hexavalent chromium , and nickel  
compounds . Therefore, this project is subject to the requirements of Regulation 2 -5.  In addition, this 
project is located within an overburdened community (OBC) as defined by Regulation 2 -1-243 and will 
only be allowed an acceptable project cancer risk of 6.0 in a million.

Regulation 1: General Provisions and Definitions  
The facility is subject to Regulation 1, Section 301, which prohibits discharge of air contaminants 
resulting in public nuisance.  The facility is expected to comply with this requirement.  
 
Regulation 2, Rule 2: Permits – New Source Review  
 
Best Available Control Technology:   Per Regulation 2 -2-301, Best Available Control Technology 
(BACT) is required for new or modified sources with highest day emissions of 10 pounds per day or  more 
of POC, NPOC, NO x, PM 10, PM 2.5, SO 2, and/or CO. S -23 does not have a BACT pollutant PTE equal to 
or greater  than 10 pounds per highest day . Therefore, the source is not subject to BACT . 
 
Emission Offsets:   
Under Section 2 -2-302, emission offsets are required for new or modified sources at a facility which 
emits or will be permitted to emit 10 tons per year or more of POC and NO X. The facility will not have a 
PTE more than 10 tons per year of POC or NO X. Therefore, the facility is not subject to the offset  
requirements of Regulation 2 -2-302. 
 
Under Section 2 -2-303, emission offsets are required for new or modified sources at a facility which 
emits or will be permitted to emit 10 0 tons per year or more o f PM2.5, PM 10, and SO 2. The facility will not 
have a PTE more than 100 tons per year of PM 2.5, PM 10, and SO 2. Therefore, the facility  is not subject to 
the offset  requirements of Regulation 2 -2-303. 
 
Under Section 2 -2-304 Federal P revention of Significant Deterioration  (PSD)  , BACT is required for each 
PSD pollutant for which the net increase in emissions from the PSD Project will be significant as defined 
under Section 2 -2-227. The facility  is not subject to PSD permitting requirements under Regulation 2 -2-
304.
