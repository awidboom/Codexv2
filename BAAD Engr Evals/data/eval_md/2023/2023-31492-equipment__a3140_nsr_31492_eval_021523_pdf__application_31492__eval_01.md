Cintas launders textiles from a variety of customers, which typically consist of 
commercial and institutional facilities.  The textiles that are processed at the facility 
comprise of mats, towels, mops, and garments.  The washing operation is water-based 
and does not rely on solvents for cleaning.  Washed textiles are then dried in the natural 
gas fired dryers.  In addition, Cintas requests textile characteristics from customers to 
ensure that no solvent is released from the washing and drying operations. 
 
In the past, Cintas had the capability of processing solvent-laden materials with the 
assistance of an aboveground storage tank (S-2), a filter press surge tank (S-4), a 
waste petroleum distillate storage tank (S-5), a series of Petro-Misers (S-8 through S-
12), and washers (S-16 through S-21).  However, the aforementioned sources have 
been decommissioned and the operation has changed so that solvent-laden textiles are 
now processed offsite.  However, Cintas retains the ability of processing solvent-laden 
textiles if source testing is performed and the facility adheres to the limits within the 
assigned permit condition.  Although, processing solvent-laden textiles will be difficult 
without Petro-Misers; and Cintas must apply for permits for any new Petro-Misers. 
 
In addition to the washing and drying operations, Cintas collects solvent-laden textiles 
from customers.  The solvent-laden textiles are collected in closed container bags off-
site.  Once the closed container bags arrive on-site, the closed container bags are 
delivered into Department of Transportation (DOT) approved shipping containers.  At no 
time are the bags of solvent-laden textiles opened to prevent the release of volatile 
organics.  The bagged solvent-laden textiles are then shipped to Phase II Industrial 
Laundry Services, Inc. in Tijuana, Mexico for processing.  Once the solvent-laden 
textiles have been cleaned, the textiles are shipped back to the facility for redistribution.

NOx and POC 
Cintas has previously accepted a permit condition which limits the facility’s PTE to less 
than 35 tons of POC.  Therefore, any new POC emission increase will be provided offsets 
from the District’s Small Facility Banking Account.  Furthermore, the facility is not 
expected to exceed 10 tons of NOx per year and is not subject to the NOx offset 
requirement of Regulation 2-2-302.  The increase in POC emissions from S-27 will add to 
the facility PTE but will remain under 35 tons/year.  The proposed POC emissions 
increase from S-27 will be provided offsets from the District’s Small Facility Banking 
Account, unless the District’s Small Facility Banking Account has been exhausted, at 
which point, the facility will be required to provide offsets. 
 
PM10 and SO 2 
This plant is not a major facility because it’s potential to emit for PM 2.5, PM10, or SO 2 after 
S-27 is permitted will be less than 100 tons per year per pollutant.  Therefore, PM 10/PM2.5 
and SO 2 emission offsets are not required.
