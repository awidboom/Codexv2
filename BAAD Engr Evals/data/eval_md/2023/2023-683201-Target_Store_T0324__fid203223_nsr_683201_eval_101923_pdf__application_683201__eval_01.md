## CEQA
JSONPath: `$.CEQA.narrative`

California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA)
A)  
This project includes an internal combustion diesel engine that will drive  a fire-pump  that 
may be employed to mitigate fires and prevent or reduce damage to life, health and 
property.  
  
The 2023 C alifornia Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Statute and Guidelines states in 
Section 15359 that an “Emergency” means a sudden, unexpected occurrence, involving a 
clear and imminent danger, demanding immediate action to prevent or mitigate loss of, or 
damage  to, life, health, property, or essential public services. “Emergency” includes such 
occurrences as fire, flood, earthquake, or other soil or geologic movements, as well as 
such occurrences as riot, accident, or sabotage. Therefore, the operation of S -1 falls under 
this definition.  
  
Furthermore, Article 18, Statutory Exemptions, Section 15269(c) of the CEQA State 
Guidelines states that specific actions necessary to prevent or mitigate an emergency are 
exempt from CEQA review.  
  
Since this project is statutorily exempt, it is not subject to the CEQA review requirement 
of Regulation 2 -1-310, and no further CEQA analysis is required.
