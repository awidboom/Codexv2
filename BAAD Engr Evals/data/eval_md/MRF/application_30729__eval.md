Background
nd  
 
Tesoro Refining & Marketing Company LLC (Tesoro)  has applied  for a Permit to Operate under the 
Accelerated Permitting Program for the  following  equipment : 
 S-1401 S ulfur Recovery Unit (3-Stage),
 abated by A -1402 , SCOT Tail Gas Unit  and A -1525  Tail Gas 
Incinerator  
 
The Sulfur Recovery Unit ( SRU ) was initially operated  in 1963 by Monsanto Chemical Plant , permitted by 
Tosco Corporation in 1984, and now owned and operated by Tesoro . The SRU is equipped with a SCOT 
Tail Gas Unit (A -1402) to treat the tailgas from the Claus portion of the SRU. The treated tailgas fr om the 
SRU is incinerated in the Tail Gas Incinerator (A -1525), which consists of three John Zink Burners  and is 
located at the base of the SRU stack . A-1525 operates  at approximately 1,200ºF , mainly converting 
hydrogen sulfide (H 2S) to sulfur dioxide (SO 2). The SRU incinerator combusts 100 psia refinery fuel gas as 
primary fuel and natural gas as a backup. The destruction efficiency is expected to be 98% or better, with a minimum operating temperature of 1,200ºF. 
 
On April 4, 2020, Tesoro was temporarily i dled due to reduced transportation gasoline fuel demand 
resulting from COVID -19. The intent was to resume crude oil processing operations when economic 
conditions improved; however, on August 3, 2020, Tesoro announced various units within the refinery would be indefinitely idled as the facility transitions toward renewable fuels production. As part of the 
gasoline refining decommission process, Tesoro will need t o process the remaining sour water at the 
facility  and will be operating at a much lower sulfur production capacity of less than 30 tons/day. Tesoro 
currently has approximately 148,000 barrels of sour water to process, which is expected to take 20 days.  
See Figure 1 for the sour water process flow diagram.  
 Currently, S-1401 has a grandfathered sulfur production limit of 200 tons/day and an emission limit of 4 lbs 
of SO
2 per ton of sulfur produced as specified in Permit Condition 267, Part 2  and Permit Condition 8077, 
Part B9.  Because the SRU will be operated at a much lower production capacity (less than 30 tons/day of 
sulfur production) for this activity, compliance with the production -based limit (4 lbs/ton SO 2) is not 
achievable. However, total mass emission from this activity will be significantly lower than normal operation  of up to 200 tons/day. Tesoro began processing sour water on August 31, 2020 and ceased 
operation on September 5, 2020 as the SO
2 emission limit approached 4 lbs/ton. See Figure 2 for data 
collected during the sour water process. As a result, Tesoro is requesting a change in the production- based 
limit to reflect these lower production levels during this decommissioning process.  
 
Due to the urgency of process ing the remaining sour water and have the SRU/SCOT unit de -inventoried 
and cleaned prior to October 29, 2020, Tesoro has requested a temporary permit to operate pursuant to 
Regulation 2 -1-302.2 .3, Accelerated Permitting Program , as this change is considered an alteration to an 
existing source . Tesoro has accepted lower SO 2, H2SO 4 emission and fuel throughput limits  when 
producing less than 30 tons of sulfur  per day than its normal permitted operation of up to 200 tons /day to 
ensure this process does not result in an increase in criteria pollutant and toxic emissions. A t emporary 
permit to operate wa s issued on September  17, 2020.

Toxic Air Contaminants  (TACs) 
The majority of the TACs are generated in the combustion process at the SRU (S -1401), SCOT Tail Gas 
Unit (A -1402), and SRU Stack Incinerator (A -1525). S -1401 and A- 1402 combust natural gas, and A- 1525 
combusts refinery fuel gas as primary fuel and natural gas as backup. Since the facility has been idled and 
no longer produce refinery fuel gas, only natural gas is used  for the decommissioning process . 
 Tesoro has accepted the following throughput limit to ensure TACs will not increase on either an hourly or 
annual basis during the process ing of sour water  at less than 30 tons/day of sulfur production:

Sulfuric Acid Mist  (H2SO 4) 
Per Permit Condition 19528, Part 9, Tesoro is required to conduct annual source testing for S -1401 to 
measure its SO 3 and H 2SO 4 emission rate. Previous source tests were conducted during normal refinery 
operation at sulfur production rates of approximately 160 tons/day.  When operating at lower sulfur 
production rates (less than 30 tons/day), SO 2 emissions  are expected to decrease, thereby expecting a 
decrease in SO 3 and H 2SO 4 emissions. Tesoro has accepted the highest mass emission rate of 2.58 lbs/hr of 
H2SO 4 from source test results to ensure there is no increase in sulfuric acid mist during the

Finding of and Citation to Basis for Exemption:  The Air District has determined that the permit 
action for Source 1401  is categorically exempt from CEQA per Air District Regulation 2 -1-312.6, 
“Permit applications relating exclusively to the repair, maintenance or minor  alteration of existing 
facilities, equipment or sources involving negligible or no  expansion of use beyond that previously 
existing ,” and Air District Regulation 2-1- 312.11, “ Projects at an existing stationary source for 
which there will be no net  increase in the emissions of air contaminants from the stationary source 
and for which there will be no other significant environmental  effect,” and therefore exempt from 
CEQA under CEQA § 21084; Guidelines § 15301.  This action is further exempt under the 
“common sense” exemption. (Guidelines § 15061(b)(3)).  
Reasons for Exemption:  The Air District’s permit action is exempt because it permits only a 
minor alteration , repair, or maintenance  of an existing facility, and the issuance of this permit does 
not authorize any expansion of the existing or former use of the Sulfur Recovery Unit .  Therefore, 
this permit action is subject to the “Class 1” exemption because it involves only a negligible or no expansion of the use of an existing facility’s existing or former use , Public Resources Code § 
21084; CEQA Guidelines § 15301.  In addition, the applicant has included in its permit application CEQA -related information (CEQA Appendix H) that demonstrates with certainty that the project 
has no potential for resulting in any additional or different environmental impacts beyond what is 
already entailed in the applicant’s existing use of the source; the project would therefore also be 
subject to the “Common Sense” exemption.
