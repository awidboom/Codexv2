BACKGROUND
This application is to amend a permit condition to the Permit to Operate for the Tesoro Refining 
and Marketing Company (Tesoro) for the following equipment: 
 
S-612 Tank A-612; Internal Floating Roof, Capacity:  420K Gallons, Storing:  Gasoline and Ethyl Alcohol 
 
In 1992, the owner of the refinery, Tosc o, received the Authority to Construct to install new seals at S-612.  The 
authority to construct authorized only storage of gasolin e.  During the issuance of the Permit to Operate, the 
condition was administratively changed to allow storage of other materials, subject to review and approval by the 
District.    Tosco explicitly requested approval to st ore ethyl alcohol (ethanol ) in February 1992.  Alt hough there is no District 
document indicating approval, Ken Lim recalls the discussi on between the District and Tosco, which alos involved 
Bill DeBoisblanc. At that time, the criteria applied by the District in reviewion material substitutions were volatility and toxicity. Ethanol is less volatile and less toxic than gasoline, and woul d have been approve d without further 
review under the criteria then being applied.    After 1992, the Permits to Operate allow for “multi-liquid” storage and Tosco ha s stored ethanol at S-612 since that 
time.  The language of condition 6740 part 4 was never updated to reflect this usag e. It is being updated as part of 
the current application.  
In condition 6740 part 4, Tesoro is allowed to store up to 243,000 barrels per year gasoline.  The 
permit condition will be amended to explicitly state that ethyl alcohol is also allowed to be stored at S-612.  The ethanol is used as a fuel-blending component.  The MSDS shows that the ethanol can contain up to 5% unleaded gasoline.BACKGROUND
