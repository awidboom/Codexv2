 Due to a rapid and significant decline in demand for transportation fuels following the onset of the COVID‐
19 pandemic, Marathon  idled the Martinez refinery in April 2020. In August 2020, Marathon  announced its 
intent to convert the Martinez refinery to rene wable  fuels production. On September 30, 2020, Marathon  
submitted to the Air District an application for an authority to construct that would authorize the alterations necessary to accomplish the conversion of the facility to renewable fuels production. While the Martinez 
refinery is idled and is not currently processing crude oil to manufacture transportation fuels, certain 
permitted operations continue and have continued since petroleum refining operations were idled in April 2020. Specifically, the refin ery continues to conduct permitted activities, including loading and unloading 
of product, vapor recovery, wastewater treatment and flaring.  
 Finished petroleum products are received via ship through the Avon Wharf Berth 1A (S‐1560). Gasoline 
and diesel fuel are stored in various existing storage tanks. These products are loaded into trucks via the 
Truck Loading Rack (S‐1025). One of the compressors at No. 1 Gas Plant Vapor Recovery is operational to provide vacuum for the Vapor Recovery tanks and gasoline loading rack. The Waste Water System (S‐819, 
S‐830, S‐831, S‐842, and S‐1026) has remained online to eliminate toxics and pollutants prior to 
discharging into Suisun Bay. Vapor recovery gases are captured by No. 1 Gas Plant and then sent to the 
flare system, normally sent to refinery process heaters (S-908, S -909, and S -912) to combust the recovered 
 
gas. Four flares East Air (S‐854), Emergency Flare (S‐992), West Air (S‐1012), the Coker Flare (S‐1517) 
are operational at this  time.  
 
On May 26, 2021, the Air District issued Notice of Violation Nos. A58276, A25275, A58274, A58273, 
A58272, and A56448 for continuous flaring  during refinery idling.  
 
On July 14, 2021, the Air District authorized the use of Envent’s Portable Thermal Oxidizer (Plant #16338, 
A-23, Permitted under Application No. 22751) at Marathon for the purpose of controlling vapors as 
outlined in Figure 1. Permit Condition #24839, Part 5, has a throughput limit of 249,000 therms  
(24,500,000 cubic feet) of natural gas during any consecutive 12 -month period. 
 
Marathon is estimating that the throughput limit will be reached by November 5, 2021. As such, Marathon 
is proposing to obtain a permanent permit to operate Envent’s portable thermal oxidizer  connected to the 
A-14 vapor recovery system when refinery process heaters are out of service. A- 1584 will be assigned 
under Plant #14628 for this thermal oxidizer.

pounds or more per highest day of POC, NPOC, NOx, CO, SO 2, PM 10 or PM 2.5.

 As shown in Table 2, A -1584 has a potential to emit one or more criteria pollutant in an amount of 10.0 or 
more pounds on any day; however, A- 1584 is exempt from BACT requirements per Regulation 2 -2-102. 
As shown below, A- 1584 is being used to control  odors from sources , to comply with BACT requirements 
for sources subject to BACT, and to comply with Air District Rules.  Best Available Retrofit Control 
 
Technology (BARCT)  is defined in Section 40406 of the  California Health and Safety Code as an emission 
limitation that  is based on the maximum degree of reduction achievable, taking into account  environmental, 
energy, and economic impacts by each class or category of source. During the rule development process, 
Air District staff proposes an emission level to t he Board based on a level that can be achieved by means of 
a technology or best practices, or other means which considers environmental benefits (emissions reductions) and impact (CEQA analyses); energy (also evaluated under CEQA); and economic impacts (cost, cost effectiveness, incremental cost effectiveness, and socioeconomic impacts ), all of which the 
Board considers when adopting a proposed rule or amendments to a rule.  Therefore, all Air District Rules 
are considered BARCT Rules. In addition, Air District Rules referenced below have a rule stringency of 
BARCT on the California Air Resources Board website. As such, A -1584 is an abatement device used to 
comply with BACT and BARCT requirements and meets the exemption requirements per Regulation 2 -2-
102.
