 Envent  offers services of renting storage tanks for temporary storage of waste waters containing organic 
liquids such as gasoline, crude oil, jet fuel, diesel, and oily sludge. Storage tanks S -19 and S -20 abated by 
A-19 are currently operated at Tesoro Refining & Marketing Company LLC (Tesoro), Amorco Wharf, 
Plant #14629 : 1750 Marina Vista Avenue, Martinez, CA . See Figure 1 for process flow diagram.  
 
The vapor control system (A-19) consists of two 1000- lb activated carbon vessels in series connected to the 
headspace groundwater storage tanks S -19 and S -20. The inlet, midpoint between the two carbon vessels, 
and outlet concentrations have been monitored daily with a photoionization de tector (PID) since the system 
was installed in 2009 in accordance with Permit Condition 24010, Part 5.   
 The monitoring readings are used to predict the time of organic breakthrough in order to maintain compliance with Permit Condition 24010, Parts 6 and 7 , which require  activated carbon replacement when 
the outlet concentration exceeds 10% of the inlet or 10 ppm, whichever is greater.  
 On April 8, 2020, Envent requested a change  to the monitor ing frequency from daily to weekly for A- 19. 
On May 2, 2020, the  Air District granted a temporary change in monitoring frequency to weekly for three 
months  to Permit Condition 24010, Part 5, under the condition that Envent formalize s the request through a 
permit application no later than August 1, 2020.  Envent  submitted this application on July 27, 2020 to 
permanently change the monitoring frequency from daily to weekly.  
 
Monitoring data from January 2017 to March 2020 can be found in Appendix A. As shown, the last carbon 
change -out occurred on March 14, 2018. The midpoint concentrations started  to increase around September 
27, 2017 and continue d to rise into March 2018 due to the depletion of carbon capacity in the lead vessel. 
After the carbon change -out, midpoint concentrations declined as expected.  
 Since th e last carbon change -out in 2018, carbon replacement has not been needed primarily due to the 
shutdown of ABW -EW02 pump on January 31, 2019.  The groundwater treatment system influent 
concentrations are shown in Figure 2 and reflected in the inlet monitorin g data in Appendix A. As shown, 
the influent concentrations have decreased significantly after the shutdown of ABW -EW02, which reduces 
the use of the carbon adsorption system. 
 The carbon system is equipped with two carbon vessel s in series . When a breakthrough (concentration is 
exceeding 10% of the inlet or 10 ppm) is identified at the outlet of  the lead vessel, the lead vessel will be 
changed.  During carbon change -out, the lead canister is removed and replaced with fresh carbon. The 
second carbon canister becomes the lead vessel and the carbon canister with fresh carbon becomes the lag 
canister. Therefore, the second carbon vessel is always equipped with fresh carbon to ensure outlet 
concentrations do not exceed 10% of the inlet or 10 ppm. Outlet concentrations are shown in Appendix A.  
The maximum recorded outlet concentration during this span is 0.3 ppm.  
 
 
Application 30642 – Page 2 of 7 In comparison to weekly monitoring data  as shown in Figure 3, measured concentrations are consistent 
with daily monitoring data.  Envent us es a MiniRae 3000 PID with a maximum detection limit of 15,000 
ppm, which explains why the maximum reading  at the inlet is consistently at 15,000  ppm. While actual 
inlet concentrations are expected to be higher than 15,000 ppm, the midpoint and outlet conc entrations are 
used to identify breakthroughs. The inlet concentration is used to establish the threshold for change -out 
when the outlet concentration exceeds 10% of the inlet concentration. Therefore, it is not necessary to use a 
wider range instrument to  measure concentrations greater than 15,000 ppm as a lower  recorded  inlet 
concentration  is more conservative.  
 
In addition, the Air District requested that the flowrate through A -19 be measured and recorded at the time 
the monitoring in Permit Condition 24010, Part 5, is done. Envent has provided flowrate data with weekly monitoring data to ensure measured concentr ations are representative of vapor flow from S -19 and S -20 as 
opposed to background concentrations. Background measurements are taken from ambient air around the carbon abatement canisters and are consistently measured at 0 ppm. The groundwater extraction tanks are 
closed systems that vent through the carbon canister system and fugitive emissions from equipment leaks 
are minimal. Furthermore, the carbon system is located in a remote area where background measurements 
of 0 ppm are reasonable.  
 
Envent has pro vided sufficient monitoring data indicating that weekly monitoring is adequate to identify 
breakthrough in the lead vessel while maintaining compliance via adsorption capacity in the lag vessel.  
 Lastly, the Air District issued an Authority to Construct on  September 26, 2019 to utilize S -20 in Tesoro’s 
new multi -phase extraction  system at the Amorco Terminal under Application 29669. Following the 
implementation of  this project, the ownership of S -20 will be transferred to Tesoro (Plant #14629) and will 
be permitted as S -60. S-60 will continue weekly monitoring in accordance with Permit Condition #27069, 
Part 8. Therefore, this change in monitoring frequency will not impact Application 29669.
