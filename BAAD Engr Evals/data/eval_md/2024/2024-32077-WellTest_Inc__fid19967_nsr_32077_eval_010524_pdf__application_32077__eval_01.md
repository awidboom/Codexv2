WellTest, Inc. has requested a Change of Condition to an existing Permit to Operate for a portable 
soil vapor extraction (SVE) unit to allow the equipment to be operated at 2910 Almaden 
Expressway, San Jose, CA 95125, which is within 1,000 feet of K-12 schools.  The equipment 
associated with this project is described below: 
 
S-4  Portable Soil Vapor Extraction System (vacuum pump and ancillary), Rietschle, 
 C-VLR-500, 353 cfm 
 
Abated by

 at least), Baker, Filtration Kleen Air 55R or equivalent.                                
 
The exiting S-4 Portable Soil Vapor Extraction (SVE) system will be used to clean out the soil and 
soil vapor at a former dry cleaner site located on 2910 Almaden Expressway, San Jose, CA 95125-
4214. The site is significantly impacted with POC. GeoTracker site on California Government 
webpage lists the site as a “Cleanup Program Site”.  
(GeoTracker Link: 
https://geotracker.waterboards.ca.gov/map/?CMD=runreport&myaddress=2910+Almaden+Expr
essway%2C+San+Jose%2C+CA+95125-4214 ) 
 
This portable soil vapor extraction unit consists of a vacuum blower (S-4) with a maximum 
capacity of 353 cfm. Extracted soil vapors will be directed to granulated activated carbon 
adsorption vessels in series (A-4) with 99% abatement efficiency for treatment prior to discharge 
to the atmosphere. 
 
The main compound found in the soil was perchloroethylene (PCE). Emissions calculations were 
based on laboratory results from samples taken at the site. No emissions are expected to trigger 
Health Risk Assessment (HRA) per Regulation 2-5, Table 1. Startup TO-15 test will be required

## BACT
JSONPath: `$.BACT.text`

Best Available Control Technology (BACT)
(BACT) 
In accordance with Regulation 2-2-301, Best Available Control Technology (BACT) is triggered 
for any new or modified source with the potential to emit 10 pounds or more per highest day of 
POC, NPOC, nitrogen oxides (NO x), carbon monoxide (CO), sulfur dioxides (SO 2), particulate 
matter less than 10 micrometer (PM 10) and particulate matter less than 2.5 micrometer (PM 2.5). 
 
S-4 for this project is not a new or modified source.  Therefore, BACT requirements do not apply.

Offsets
Pursuant to Regulation 2-2-302, offsets must be provided for any new or modified source at a 
facility that emits, or is permitted to emit, more than 10 tons per year of precursor organic 
compounds (POCs) or nitrogen oxides (NO X).  Furthermore, pursuant to Regulation 2-2-303 
offsets must be provided for any new or modified source at a major facility with a cumulative 
increase that exceeds 1.0 ton per year of PM 10, PM2.5, or sulfur dioxide (SO 2). 
 
The site is not expected to have a PTE greater than 10 tons per year of POC or NO X, nor is the site 
a major site of PM 10, PM2.5, and SO 2.  Therefore, the requirements of Regulations 2-2-302 and 2-
2-303 do not apply.
