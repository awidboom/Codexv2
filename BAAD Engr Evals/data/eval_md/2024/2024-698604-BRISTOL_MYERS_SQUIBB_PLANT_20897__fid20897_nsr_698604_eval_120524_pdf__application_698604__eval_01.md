cleaning emissions will be emitted fugitively  (i.e. will not be routed through an abatement unit prior to atmospheric 
discharge) . Therefore , for purposes of emission calculation s, solvent usage is assumed  to be equal to solvent  
emissions.

## toxic_risk_screening_analysis
JSONPath: `$.toxic_risk_screening_analysis.narrative`

Toxic Health Risk Assessment
Based on the calculations presented in Table 2, TAC emissions e xceed the Air District’s Risk Screening trigger level  
for acute emissions  set forth in Reg ulation  2-5, Table 1.  Therefore, a n HRA was required  per Regulation 2 -5-110. 
 
The Air District therefore undertook an HRA to evaluate the potential acute and chronic, non -carcinogenic health 
risks from emissions from this project. Since the only TAC that this source emits is IPA, and IPA is not a 
carcinogen, cancer risks were not evaluated. The HRA evaluated risks to workers, residents, and students in the 
vicinity of the project.  
 
Results from the HRA indicate that the maximum project chronic hazard index is 0.00024 , and the project maximum 
acute hazard index is 0.16. 
 
This project is located in an overburdened community (OBC), as defined by Regulation 2 -1-243; therefore, the 
project must comply with the project cancer risk limit of 6 in a million. Since the estimated project cancer risk does 
not exceed 6 in a million and project hazard indices do not exceed 1.0, this project complies with the Air District’s 
Regulation 2 -5-302 project risk requirements.  
 
Compliance with Regulation 2 -5 is therefore satisfied.

California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA)
A)  
 
This permit application is not subject to the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) because the Air 
District’s evaluation is a ministerial action (Public Resources Code Section 21080(b)(1) and CEQA Guidelines 
Section 15268(a)) conducted using the fixed standards and objective measurements in the Air District’s rules and 
regulations.  
 
Air District Rules and Regulations  
 
Wipe cleaning operations are subject to the storage and disposal requirements of Regulation 8 -1-320, 321, and 322 
and the recordkeeping requirements of Regulation 8 -16-501 and Regulation 8 -4-501.  
 
In addition, each wipe cleaning operation is subject to either 1)  Regulation 8 -4-302.1 (< 5 TPY/source) OR 2) 
Regulation 8 -4-302.2 (85% overall control) AND 3) the evaporative loss minimization requirements of Regulation 
8-4-312. Because the proposed wipe cleaning operations at S-12 will result in emissions < 5 TPY, the applicant will 
satisfy with Regulation  8-4-302.1.  
 
Moreover, wipe cleaning operations for 1) the cleaning of surfaces prior to Regulation 8 -4 coating operations OR 2) 
further treatment, sale, or intended use are subject to the VOC standards of Regulation 8 -4-313, unless specifically 
exempt by Regulation 8 -4-116. The applicant is eligible for the limited exemption from 8 -4-313 per 8-4-116 because 
the proposed surface preparation and cleaning operations are associated with biomedical research and development 
operations.  
 
As stated previously in this evaluation, the project is located within (i) 1,000 feet from the nearest school  (Hoover 
Elementary)  and (ii) an overburdened community and requires an HRA. Therefore, a public noti ce will be 
distributed and a thirty -day public comment period will be initiated.
