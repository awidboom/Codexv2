BACKGROUND
State Bar of California (State Bar) has applied for an Authority to Construct for the following source:  
S-3 Emergency Standby Diesel Generator Set 
Make: Cummins, Model: QSX15-G9, Model Year: 2023 
755 bhp, 30.3 gal/hr, 4.15 MMBtu/hr

A-1 Diesel Particulate Filter 
The Emergency Standby Diesel Engine Generator Set (S-3) will be located at 180 Howard Street in San Francisco, 
CA 94105. The engine will be used to provide power to the facility in case of an emergency. The engine for S-3 
is certified to meet the Environmental Protection Agency and California Air Resources Board (EPA/CARB) Tier 2 
standard. The engine will burn commercially available California low sulfur diesel fuel. The sulfur content of the 
diesel fuel will not exceed 0.0015% by weight.

General (2-5-100)  
Diesel particulate emissions from the planned operation of S-3 are calculated to be 1.21 lb/year which exceeds 
the trigger level of 0.26 lb/year. Therefore, S-3 is subject to the requirements of this regulation and a health risk 
analysis (HRA). As such, this application requires a Toxics Risk Screening Analysis. Regulation 2-5 requires that the 
cumulative impacts from all related projects permitted within the last five years be included in the risk screening 
analysis. There are no related projects within the last five years.  
 
The results of the HRA were: a Cancer Risk of  0.50 in a million, and a Chronic Hazard Index (HI) of  0.00038. In 
accordance with the District’s Regulation 2-5-301, S-3 is not required to meet TBACT because the source cancer 
risk does not exceed 1.0 in a million and the chronic HI does not exceed 0.20.  Since the estimated project cancer 
risk does not exceed 10.0 in a million and chronic hazard index does not exceed 1.0, this project complies with the 
District’s Regulation 2-5-302 project risk requirements, for projects not located in an Overburdened Community, 
as defined in Regulation 2-1-243.   
Regulation 6 - Particulate Matter, Rule 1 - General Requirements 
Ringelmann No. 2 Limitation (Section 6-1-303) 
Since S-3 has a displacement of 912 in3 it is subject to Regulation 6-1-303.  The engine is expected to meet this 
requirement with regular maintenance and inspection.  
Visible Particles (Section 6-1-305) 
Since S-3 will emit a very small amount of PM 10 it is not expected to produce visible emissions or fallout in violation 
of this regulation and will be assumed to be in compliance with Regulation 6-1-305  pending a regular inspection. 
Total Suspended Particulate Concentration Limits (Section 6-1-310) 
Per Regulation 6-1-310.2 , a person shall not emit from any source particulate matter in excess of 0.117 grains/dscf 
of exhaust gas volume if the exhaust gas rate is between 2,649 cfm and  3,531 cfm.  The exhaust rate for this engine 
is 3,105 cfm. The emission rate from S-3 is 0.015 grams/bhp-hr, which results in an outlet grain loading of 0.0023 
grains/dscf at 0% O 2. The emission rate is less than the limit 0.117 grains/dscf and is in compliance with Regulation 
6-1-310.

Offset Requirements, POC and NO x (Section 2-2-302) 
This section establishes emission offset requirements for POC and NO x at facilities that will have the potential to 
emit more than 10 tons per year of POC or NO x. If the facility will have the potential to emit more than 10 tons 
per year but less than 35 tons per year of NO x or POC after the new or modified source is constructed, offsets must 
be provided at a 1:1 ratio for any un-offset cumulative increase in emissions at the facility. These offsets shall be 
provided by the District’s Small Facility Banking Account unless the applicant owns offsets. If the facility emits 
more than 10 tons of POC and/or NO x per year, offsets must be provided. Based on potential to emit, the POC and 
NOx emissions will not exceed 10 tpy. Therefore, the engine is not subject to the offset requirements of Regulation 
2-2-302.

### General
STATEMENT OF COMPLIANCE:
Regulation 2- Permits, Rule 1 – General Requirements 
Ministerial Projects (Section 2-1-311) 
As per CEQA Article 18, Section 15301, this project qualifies for a categorical exemption as a project with involving 
negligible or no expansion of use. One of the ways a project can qualify for this categorical exemption is if the 
addition to existing structures do not result in an increase of more than; “50% of the floor area of the structures 
before the addition, or 2,500 square feet, whichever is less” or “10,000 square feet if … the area in which the 
project is not environmentally sensitive”. According to the facility’s Appendix H form, the project will be 
approximately 272 square feet. Therefore, this project qualifies for the categorical exemption as per CEQA Article 
18, Section 15301.  
Public Notice, Schools and Overburdened Communities (Section 2-1-412 and California Health & Safety Code 
§42301.6) 
Pursuant to California Health & Safety Code §42301.6(a), prior to approving an application for a permit to 
construct or modification of a source, which is located within 1,000 feet from the outer boundary of a school site, 
the District shall prepare a public notice as detailed in §42301.6.  §42301.9(a) defines a “school” as any public or 
private school used for the purposes of the education of more than 12 children in kindergarten or any grades 1 to 
12, inclusive, but does not include any private school in which education is primarily conducted in private homes. 
Using the GreatSchools.org website and searching with Google Maps, it has been determined that there is one 
school located within 1,000 feet of the facility. The “Youth Chance High School” located at 169 Steuart St in San 
Francisco, is located approximately 687 feet from the facility. Therefore, a public notice is required as per 2-1-412. 
 
Pursuant to District Regulation 2-1-412, prior to approving an application for a permit to construct or modify a 
source which is located within an Overburdened Community (OBC) and for which a Health Risk Assessment is 
required pursuant to Regulation 2-5-401, the District shall prepare a public notice as detailed in §42301.6. As per 
Regulation 2-1-243, an OBC is defined as an area located within a census tract identified by the California 
Communities Environmental Health Screening Tool (CalEnviroScreen) Version 4.0, as having an overall 
CalEnviroScreen score at or above the 70th percentile or within 1,000 feet of any such census tract. The facility is 
not located in an OBC. 
Regulation 2- Permits, Rule 2 – New Source Review 
PSD Projects (Section 2-2-224) 
This section defines a PSD project as one at a facility that has the potential to emit 100 tons or more per year of 
any PSD pollutant.  This facility will not have the potential to emit 100 tons or more of any PSD pollutant therefore, 
this project is not a PSD project.  
Best Available Control Technology Requirement (Section 2-2-301)  
In accordance with Regulation 2-2-301, Best Available Control Technology (BACT) is triggered for any new or 
modified source with the potential to emit 10 or more pounds per highest day of POC, non-precursor organic 
compounds (NPOC), NO x, CO, SO 2, and/or PM 10 or PM 2.5. 
 
Based on the emission calculations provided above in “Emissions from Source S-3”, BACT is triggered for NO x and 
CO as the maximum daily emissions exceed 10 lbs/day. 
 
The District BACT/TBACT workbook has the following achieved in practice NO x and CO BACT limits for emergency 
diesel engines with a capacity between 750 and 1000 hp.  For standby diesel engines in this size range, EPA Tier 2 
standards are currently the most stringent standards that would qualify as achieved in practice BACT under the 
District program. The determination has a date of 12/22/20.

c. Hours of operation (emergency).

e. Fuel usage for each engine(s). 
[Basis: Title 17, California Code of Regulations, section 93115, ATCM for Stationary CI Engines]
