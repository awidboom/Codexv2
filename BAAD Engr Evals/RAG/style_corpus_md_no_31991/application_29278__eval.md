C.  Permit Condition  24649 for S1549 Diesel Additive Tank 
 Condition 24649 was imposed via 2010 Application 20968.  This application permitted a specific Diesel additive material storage.  Tesoro has requested that  the condition be 
revised to allow other Diesel additives.  The standard ‘other material’ language from the Permit Hand book is  added to this condition. 
 
D.  Condition 25476 for the No. 3 Reformer  
 In 2013 Permit Application 23322 No. 3 Reformer Capacity Increase, the source test requirements for the two fired heater sources (S971 and S972) were handicapped by the lack of s ource test ports.  In addition, the emissions train for the two sources was different 
than originally permitted.  Instead of the A-1433 SCR being installed on the common stack, it was installed in the S971 emissions train only and the S972 emissions discha rge 
direct to the atmosphe ric stack  without abatement.   In order to determine if S971 
operated in compliance with the applicable emissio n limits, a source test on S972 had to 
be subtracted from the source test and CEM data on the common stack.  Since the Permit to Operate was granted for the modification, Tesoro installed additional source test ports on S971.  Therefore, Condition 25476 Parts 25, 27 and 29 are  changed to reflect 
independent source tests of S971 and S972.

F.  Bubble Condition PM Source Testing  for S901 FCCU CO Boiler  
 As indicated above, Appendix C, Section 4.b of the original refinery bubble condition imposed by the No. 3 HDS project (1983 Application 27769) required PM source testing.  The requirements included source testing of the FCCU (at the stack of S9 01 FCCU CO 
Boiler), the Fluid Coker (at the stack of S903 Coker CO Boiler) and the S904 No. 6 Boiler.  A t the time, all three boilers provided abatement service as CO Boilers (S904 as a 
back -up to S903) and all three were permitted to use solid, liquid and gaseous fuel.  The 
purpose of the PM source testing was to establish a PM emission factor for a variety  of 
operation conditions for the three sources.  The emission factors would either confirm or adjust the initial emission factors specified in Appendix C.  The frequency of the PM source testing was specified as monthly, every other year. 
 In 2005 an Abate ment Order (Docket 3492) required Tesoro to terminate operation of the 
Fluid Coker and in 2009 Tesoro was granted a Permit to Operate for the replacemen t 
Delayed Coker.  In 2008, Tesoro submitted Application 19300 to remove the provisions for S904 to provi de back -up CO Boiler service for the decommissioned Fluid Coker.  In 
addition, Condition 7397 only allows S904 to burn gaseous fuels.  Therefore, the mo nthly 
PM source testing every other year only applies to S901. 
 Tesoro has proposed that this PM source testing requirement for S901 be deleted.  The testing has resulted in decades of test data, and there is no value for more test data.  In  
addition, all of the testing is for ‘front half’ filterable PM10 using EPA Method 5.  Since 
PM
2.5 is now a criteria po llutant, PM 2.5 testing, including the condensable portion, is the 
current focus for FCCUs.   Tesoro has agreed to perform a series of PM 2.5 source testi ng.  
Details of this agreement can be found in NSR Permit Application 29601.  In addition, proposed amendments to Regulation 6, Rule 5 will require periodic PM
2.5 source testing.  
 Therefore, removal of the PM
10 source testing for the FCCU is  justified.
