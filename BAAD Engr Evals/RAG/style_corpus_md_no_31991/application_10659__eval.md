BACKGROUND
The Tesoro Refining and Marketing Company (Tesoro) is  applying for a change in permit conditions for the 
Authority to Construct and Permit to Operate the following equipment: 
 
S-1496 Fixed Roof Tank; Tank A-876, Capacity:  80,000 Ba rrels, Storing:  Heavy Reformate with Pentanes, 
Straight Run Heavy Naphtha abated  by A-14 Vapor Recovery System 
 
Tesoro would like to clarify Permit Condition 21100, Part 1 to show that the limit of 2,500,000 barrels applies to the 
“net” versus “total” throughput of the tank.  Emissions from S-1496 were calculated using the EPA Tanks 4.0 program.  The Tanks 4.0 program uses “Net Throughput” or the “annual net throughput in gallons per year”  (from User’s Guide to Tanks, September 30, 1999).  If the conditi on is not changed to reflect  the “net” throughput, then 
the emissions associated with S-1496 need to be modified.  According to the User’s Guide to Tanks, for a tank that is operated with little or no change in the liquid level, “t he inputs to the working loss equation should be modified 
for constant level tanks since the working losses are based on changes in the height of the liquid”.  The annual tank 
turnover rate must be adjusted to prevent overestimati on of working losses.  Instead of updating emissions, the 
conditions will be clarified to speci fy the ‘net’ throughput limit of the ta nk.  S-1496 is equipped with a radar-
monitoring device that continuously monitors the level of liquid in the tank.  From the continuous level readings, Tesoro can calculate the change in liquid height and determine the “net ” throughput through the tank.  Condition 
21100 will be updated to clarify that the limit of 2,500,000 ba rrels per year applies to the “net” throughput of S-
1496.  There will be no increase or change in emissi ons with the clarification of the permit condition.BACKGROUND

ANCE 
The owner/operator of S-1496 Fixed Volume Portable Tank is subject to Regulation 8-5-301 Storage Tank Control 
Requirements.  For tanks greater than 150 m3 (39,626 gallons ) with organic contents with a true vapor pressure 
greater or equal to 11 psia, a pressure tank or approved em ission control system is required.  The owner/operator of 
S-1496 is subject to Regulation 8-5-306 Requirements for Approved Emission Control Systems.  The owner/operator will be required to ab ate S-1496 with A-14 Vapor Recovery Sy stem at all times.  The A-14 Vapor 
Recovery System and process heaters will be conditionally permitted to provi de an abatement efficiency of at least 
99.5% by weight POC (basis:  cumulative increase, BACT, Offsets, toxics risk scr een), except as allowed by 
Regulation 8-5-328.1.2 for tank degassing.  The 99.5% dest ruction efficiency was demonstrated via source tests 
for Application #6201 (S-532 Oil Water Separator; Tank 532 abated by A-14 Vapor Recovery System).  Source 
test results were validated by the District Source Test Division (Memos from Source Test, February 26, 2004).  Annual source tests will be required to ensure a POC destru ction efficiency of at l east 99.5% by weight.  The 
owner/operator is also subject to recordkeeping (Regulation 8-5-501).
