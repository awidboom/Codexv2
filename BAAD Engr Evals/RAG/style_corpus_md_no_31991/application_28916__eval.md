Tesoro Refining & Marketing Company LLC  (Tesoro) has  applied to obtain an Authority to Construct and a Permit 
to Operate for the following equipment : 
 
S-1574   Envent Oil Water Separator T5, 200 gpm, abated by A -1574  
S-1575  Envent Recovered Oil Tank T3, 1000 gallons, abated by A -1575

S-1452  Hydrocarbon Recovery System , which includes 47 oil/water wells, and associated 
pumps (39 Light Hydrocarbon Pumps and 8 Heavy Hydrocarbon Pumps (exempt)), 
valves and flanges  
 
Tesoro refers to S -1452 as the Light non -Aqueous Phase Liquid (LNAPL) Recovery System that consists of 
groundwater wells, pumps and collection piping that is part of the Regional Water  Quality Control  Board mandated 
groundwater cleanup system.  Tesoro has fully offset the emissions from S -1452 in original permit Applications 10544 
and again when S -1452 was modified in permit Application 13240.  The total fully offset POC emissions are 2.057 
tons.  Tesoro has agreed to limit the proposed S -1452 emissions not  to exceed the fully offset emissions.  Therefore, 
this proposed project will alter S -1452.  
 
S-1574 is currently  permitted by Envent Plant 16338  as:  
 
S-41   Oil-Water Separator T-5, abated by A-7, A -10 and A -11, Activated Carbon Canisters  
 and is operated at the Tesoro Refining & Marketing Company LLC (Tesoro) Golden Eagle Refinery (Refinery) Plant 
14628.   
 The operation of this  S-41 oil-water separator (OWS) is limited to  the current groundwater remediation well flows 
that are routed to S -41, and the prop osed new flows will triple the flow to S -41.  Therefore, this project increases the 
potential to emit (PTE) of S-41 and thus  S-41 is modified .   
 
In addition to Envent S-41, Tesoro will also transfer other equipment associated with the oil -water separator (OWS) 
that is located at Tesoro from Envent to Tesoro.  This equipment currently on Envent Plant 16338 permit includes the 
following:  
 
S-39   Storage Tank  T3, 21,000 gallons, abated by A -5 and A -6, Activated Carbon Canisters  
S-40  Storage Tank  T4, 21,000 gallons, abated by A -5 and A -6, Activated Carbon Canisters  
 All of the Envent equipment was originally permitted as portable equipment.  In 2012, Envent notified the District 
that S -41 was operating at Tesoro for longer than 12- months and conseque ntly S -41 had lost its portability (i.e., had 
become a stationary source).   
 
The proposed project reroutes several groundwater remediation well flows of S -1452 from the refinery slop oil system 
to this Envent Oil -Water Separator.  The separated hydrocarbon would be sent to the slop system and the separated 
water would be sent to the refinery wastewater system.  The proposed project is shown on the flow sketch below.

Although Envent Corporation originally submitted this permit application , the District determined that the application 
should be processed as part of the Tesoro Refinery’s permit.  Tesoro agreed and requested to have permit Application 28916 issued with Tesoro as the owner/operator of the permit.  
 
The proposed project could impact the operation and emissions of the slop oil system or the wastewater treatment 
system.  Therefore, an evaluation to determine if the project results in any debottlenecking or other equipment 
modification is required.   
 
The net effect of the project is to re -route  the water -rich groundwater from the refinery slop oil system and instead 
process it in the S -1574 Oil Water Separator first.  The recovered oil from the Oil Water Separator is then sent to the 
Slop Oil System.  Therefore, from an hydrocarbon/oil standpoint, t he material processed in  the slop oil system is 
unchanged.  The water processed by the slop oil system would be reduced since water phase of the groundwater has 
been redirected to the S -1574 Oil Water Separat or where it is removed and directed to the wastewater treatment 
system.   While this water processing rate reduction could provide processing capacity for other slop oil streams, this 
project is not proposing any changes of this nature.  Furthermore, slop oil is a byproduct of the refinery operation and slop oil processing is typically more of a refinery utility rather than a managed or optimized process.  Therefore, it 
can be concluded that this project does not debottleneck the slop oil system in such a way that more or different slop 
oil can be processed.  
 
For the wastewater treatment system, the existing operation processes the water separated in the slop oil system.  This 
water stream enters the wastewater treatment system upstream of the Air Strippers S -1026.  For the proposed change, 
the portion of the water separated in the slop oil system that originates in the groundwater would be redirected to the 
S-1574 Oil Water Separator.  Once separated, it would enter the wastewater treatment system at the API 
Separator/DNF S -819.  Since S -819 is upstream of S -1026, the proposed project would increase the flow only at S -
819.  At S -1026 and downstream, the water flowrate would be unchanged.  While technically this S -819 flow increase

EMISSIONS
IONS  
 
All emissions fro m the project are POCs.  Emissions from the S-1574 OWS (T -5) and the two recovered oil tanks  S-
1575 and S -1576 (T-3, T-4) are controlled by using two carbon canisters in series.  The remaining POC emissions are 
fugitive emissions . 
 The existing Envent and the proposed Tesoro permit conditions require:  
a. The second -to-last carbon vessel to be replaced with new or downstream carbon when the non -
methane organic concentration in the effluent reaches 10% of the non- methane organic concentration entering 
the ves sel, or 10 ppmv (measured as C1), whichever is greater, [Basis: Regulation 8 -5-306] and 
b. The activated carbon in the last carbon vessel in series shall be replaced immediately with new 
carbon when the non- methane organic concentration in the effluent exc eeds 10 ppm (measured as C1). [Basis: 
Regulation 8 -5-306] 
 Tesoro is required to change the carbon in the last vessel if the 10 ppmv concentration limit is exceeded. Therefore, POC emissions are calculated based on the breakthrough limit and permit conditi on of 10 ppmv vapor concentration 
limit at the outlet of the carbon abatement  system. 
 The POC emissions limit for the S-1574 OWS is based on the design throughput capacity for the unit, 200 gpm.  The 
POC emissions limit for the S-1575 and S -1576  tanks is based on the  limited throughput of  20,000 gallons per year.  
These limits sufficiently define the PTE of the OWS and tanks .  Tesoro is required to change the carbon based on the 
monitoring of the carbon canisters.  If the 10 ppmv concentration limit is exc eeded, the carbon system must be 
changed.  So, any fluctuations or changes in hydrocarbon content of the contaminated groundwater would only effect 
the frequency of carbon canister changes and not emissi ons.  
 Compliance with the existing permit condition is completed by measuring with PID  and/or FID  at the outlet.  The 
PID/FID  is calibrated with hexane  and/or methane.

 This Oil Water Separator (OWS) source was originally permitted in 2009 Application 18873 by Envent (Plant 16338) 
as the portable source S- 41.  Envent was granted a permit and Condition 24238 limited S -41 to a throughput of 200 
gpm, 105,120,000 gal/yr, and required 3 carbon absorption systems in series to abate S -41 with at least 95% efficiency 
whenever it was operating.  Permitted abated emissions were 1051.2 lb/yr VOC and 3.15 lb/yr of benzene (the hydrocarbon material separated in S -41 was permitted to be  gasoline).  The total POC cumulative increase in 2009 for 
Envent Plant 16338 was 7.486 tons/yr so offsets did not apply.  
 On May 18, 2012 Envent notified the District that S -41 was operating at Tesoro for longer than 12-months and 
consequently S -41 had lo st its portability (i.e., had become a stationary source).  The current requirements of 
Regulation 2 -1-413, as amended December 6, 2017, specify that if a source no longer satisfies any of the requirements 
of portable permit (now called a Multiple Location  permit), including the requirement that the source not remain at 
the same facility for more than 12 consecutive months, the source will be subject to New Source Review as if it were 
a new source.  Even though these “new” requirements were clarifications of what was actually intended in the 
(previous) December 19, 2012 Rule Amendment (which in itself, as it pertained to portable/multiple facility permits, 
was also proposed as a clarification of the requirements in the previous rule), it is understandable th at an 
owner/operator would consider the language of the current Regulation 2-1- 413 as a “new” requirement.  Therefore, 
since S -41 technically lost its portability in 2012, it was agreed to require S -41 to be permitted by the refinery but not 
to subject S -1574 to the requirements of a new source.   
 Envent originally submitted this permit application as an alteration to S -41, stating that the source would not process 
more than the permitted 200 gpm of feedstock.  Tesoro also agrees with Envent since S -41 is currently permitted for 
200 gpm.  However, S -41 is not currently able to attain the 200 gpm limit.  Tesoro and Envent claim that the S -41 
design information is adequate to determine the validity of the 200 gpm limit and the S -41 potential to emit.  
Nonethe less, according to Regulation 2-1- 234.1.2, the S -41 PTE is determined by the sources actual physical ability 
to emit air pollution.  Design information is one allowable method for determining a source's potential to emit, but a reading of the entire regula tion shows the key justification is the use if "the most relevant and reliable technical 
information available regarding the source's operation..".  Material balances included in the permit application show 
current normal flow to S -41 at 9 gpm (as shown on the diagram on page  2).  Adding the 19.4 gpm of new streams to 
S-41 represents a tripling of the normal flow.  Even if one were to double the normal flowrates to represent peak 
potential flows, the existing throughput would be less than 20 gpm, and the tr ipling effect remains.  In effect, the 
groundwater remediation wells currently lined up to S -41 bottleneck the actual processing capacity that S -41 can 
achieve.  While bottlenecking is usually associated with limits of upstream or downstream units, this is  not a regulatory 
limitation.  The regulatory language states that "A source’s potential to emit shall take into account any limitation on the effective capacity of the source as a result of the capacity of any upstream or downstream process that acts as a 
“bottleneck” ..."   The remediation wells are the process of recovering groundwater and the remediation wells act as 
an upstream bottleneck.  Discussion on page 13 of the Complex Permitting Handbook for BAAQMD NSR Permitting, 
prepared in support of the rec ent Regulation 2, Rules 1 and 2 Amendments, clearly states that “the sources PTE will 
be based on its actual capacity that it can achieve in practice given the “bottleneck”.”  In other permitting situations, similar determinations have been made and the Le gal Division has been in agreement.  In conclusion , permitting the  
S-41 PTE based on 200 gpm represents an increase in emissions compared to the emissions currently achievable.  
Therefore, this project is a modification of S -41 (Tesoro’s S -1574).  
 
As summa rized above, Tesoro has suggested to permit this project as a modification to S -1574.  Tesoro was provided 
the opportunity to provide any information, in addition to that provided originally by Envent, that would support the 
project being permitted as an a lteration.  In other permit applications historical throughput information has been 
provided that demonstrates equipment has processed material at or close to the permitted capacity.  This is not a 
regulatory requirement, but information such as peak histo rical throughput information clearly demonstrates the 
attainable capacity of equipment or process units.  Tesoro/Envent chose not to provide any additional information.
