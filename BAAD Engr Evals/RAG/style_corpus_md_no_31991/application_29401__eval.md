BACKGROUND
ND  
 
The Marathon Petroleum Corporation T esoro Martinez Refinery (permitted as Tesoro Refining & Marketing 
Company LLC , hereafter Tesoro)  has applied for an Alteration for the fo llowing equipment : 
 
S-802   Fluid Catalytic Cracking Unit, adding a new “Bio- Crude” feed stream  
S-709   External Floating Roof Tank, 4,746,000 gallons, storing new FCCU feedstock  
 
As part of this project, Tesoro has applied for a Permit to Operate for the following source : 
 
S-1584   Track 4 Truck Unloading Terminal  
 This Tesoro proposed project will replace the current FCCU feed gas oil with up to 800 BPD of “Bio -Crude”.  800 
BPD represents about 1% of the 75,000 BPD permitted capacity of S -802. This new feed material originates from 
municipal solid waste, thus reducing the carbon intensity of the gasoline and diesel fuel products Tesoro markets.  The 
bio-crude is produced by Fulcrum Bioenergy, Inc.  where municipal solid waste is gasified and a fed to a Fischer -
Tropsch (FT) reactor  in a facility out- of-state.  The product “Bio -crude” (aka Fulcrum Oil, FTL, FUL -001, and 
Syncrude ) is trucked to, and unloaded at , the S -1584 truck terminal  located in Tract 4 .  From the terminal the Fulcrum 
Oil is blended with 1400 to 3200 BPD of Gas Oil to improve material handling and to comply with permitted emission 
limits .  The blended Fulcrum Oil is delivered to, and stored in  S-709.  From S -709 the new feedstock would be pumped 
to the S- 853 FCCU Feed Surge Drum, where it is sent to the S- 802 FCCU similar to other FCCU feedstock (e.g., from 
the S -850 No 3 HDS or stored in other tankage ).   
 Since the Fulcrum Oil is inherently low in sulfur content, it does not have to be hydrotreated like other FCCU 
feedstocks.  According to the Safety Data Sheet,  Fulcrum Oil can have high vapor pressures (up to 11 psia).  Tesoro 
estimates that the 20% blend Fulcrum Oil feed stock will have a ma ximum true vapor pressure of 1.17 psia.  Tesoro 
estimates that the 36% blend (800 BPD with 1400 BPD gas oil) will have a  maximum true vapor pressure of 2.81 psia.   
 
Tesoro asserts that the  new Fulcrum Oil feedstock is similar to other FCCU feedstocks does not expect any change s 
in emissions from the FCCU.  Therefore, this project does not modify the S -802 FCCU.  Per Regulation 2 -1-233, S -
802 is an altered source.  
 The new Fulcrum Oil/Gas Oil storage in S -709 may impact emissions.  S -709 was originally perm itted to store 1.5 
psia TVP crude oil in 1979 via Tosco NSR Permit Application 26763.  S -709 was permitted based on the Tosco 
emissions estimate of 5,302 lbs/yr of POC.  In order to avoid modifying S -709 with this project, Tesoro has revised 
the initial pr oposed project and will be operate the tank such that POC emissions do not exceed this permitted level.  
Per Regulation 2 -1-233, if S -709 is not modified, then S -709 is an altered source. 
 S-1584 is an existing truck terminal that has been operated by Tesoro for more than 5 years.  Tesoro has considered 
this terminal exempt from permitting.  The Fulcrum Oil is a regulated material (see vapor pressures detailed above).  Therefore S -1584 will require a permit for this proposed project.  
 
The proposed proje ct will utilize existing equipment and add new equipment.  The new equipment will allow the 
Fulcrum Oil to be unloaded from trucks, blended with gas oil (low sulfur vacuum gas oil, LSVGO) fro m S-872 
(exempt from permitting per 2-1- 123.3.2, IBP>302F), and s tored in S -709.  S -709 will be altered to insulate the tank 
and heat the stored blended material.  The existing S -709 P -9985 will be used to recirculate the stored blended material 
through a new steam exchanger to maintain temperature at 15 0 to 18 5F, and new piping and controls will be added to 
send the blended Fulcrum Oil to the S -853 FCCU Feed Surge Drum.

RACT  
 
S-802 FCCU was mod ified in 1994 Application 12722 by replacing the internal reactor riser with an external larger 
diameter riser.  To ensure the project was an alteration, S -802 was ‘baseline d’ and Conditions 11433, including mass 
emissions limits derived from the baseline emissions, was imposed.  These S -802 mass emissions limits are specified 
in Part 2 and are often referred to as the ‘mini -bubble’ to distinguish the emission limits of the Co ndition 8077 Refinery 
Emissions Cap (which also includes S -802 emissions).     
 Since 1994, there have been several actions that have required reductions in the S -802 emissions.  The most recent 
action is the latest EPA/DOJ “Consent Decree” .  In this Conse nt Decree (EPA CASE NO. SA -16-CV-00722) , S-802 
emission concentrations of NOx, SO2 and CO have been limited and are codified in Condition 11433 Parts 7, 8 and 
9.  These emissions limitations are a basis for a RACT adjustment for the ‘mini -bubble’ mass emis sion limits of 
Condition 11433 Part 2 .  However, since the permit for this project is a n urgent priority to Tesoro, it was agreed to 
address these RACT adjustments in a subsequent permit application (e.g. Application 29601 FCCU SCR Project).
