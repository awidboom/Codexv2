Background
nd  
Tesoro Refining & Marke ting Company LLC, an indirect, wholly -owned subsidiary of Marathon Petroleum 
Corporation (herein referenced as “Marathon”), has applied for a Certificate of Exemption for the following 
equipment:  
 S-646 Tank A -646, Renewable Propane Tank; 45 ,000 gallons (Regulation 2 -1-123. 3.1) 
S-647 Tank A -647, Renewable Propane Tank; 45 ,000 gallons  (Regulation 2 -1-123.3.1)  
 As part of Marathon’s Renewable Fuels Project (Application No. 30768) to convert the existing Martinez Refi nery 
from a petroleum refinery to a renewable  fuels facility, Marathon initially proposed to use seven (7) existing 
pressurized liquified petroleum gas (LPG) tanks to store renewable propane. Existing LPG tanks (S -648, S -649, S -
666, S- 667, S -668, S -669, and S- 670) were exempt from permitting per Regulation 2 -1-123.3.1 and Application No. 
30768 determined that these tanks will continue to meet exemption requirements when storing renewable 
propane. Certificates of Exemption were issued on September 13, 2022.  
 At the time of Application No. 30768, Marathon specified  that the existing pressurized LPG tanks, S -646 and S- 647, 
will be retained for refinery idling operations , i.e., equipment will not be used as part of the renewable fuels 
process. Permit Condition #27583, Part 1, was imposed to ensure sources that were not evaluated as part of 
Marathon’s Renewable Fuels Project via Application No. 30768 will not be used in the renewable fuels process. In 
addition, the permit condition requires Marathon to submit a permit application if Marathon wants to use any of 
the sources associated with the renewable fuels process.  
 Marathon is proposing to use an additional two (2 ) existing pressurized LPG  tanks (S-646 and S- 647) to store 
renewable propane. Marathon is not proposing to increase renewable propane production at the facility; 
therefore, there is no impact to the overall Renewable Fuels Project.  
 
Emissions Calculation  
Per Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) AP-42, Chapter 7, Liquid Storage Tanks , high -pressure tanks are 
considered closed systems, with virtually no emissions while fugitive losses from high -pressure tanks are estimated 
as equipment leaks. Emissions from equipment leaks are expected to be minimal and these tanks are monitored 
under the Fugitives Program. There are no direct emissions from these pressurized tanks. S- 646 and S- 647 are 
currently exempt from permitting and will continue to meet the exemption requirements pursuant to Regulation 2-1-123.3.1.
