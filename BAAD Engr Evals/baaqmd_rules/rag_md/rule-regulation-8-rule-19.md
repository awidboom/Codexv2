### 100 GENERAL

### 101 Description

**History**

- Amended 12/19/84; 10/16/02

The purpose of this Rule is to limit the emission of volatile organic compounds from the surface preparation and coating of miscellaneous metal parts and products as defined in Section 8-19-204. Any operation which is determined to be exempt from the provisions of this Rule shall be subject to the provisions of Rule 4, if not already subject to another rule of Regulation 8.

### 110 Exemption, Low Usage Coatings

**History**

- Amended 12/19/84; 12/18/85; 6/7/89; 2/3/93

in any one calendar year, provided the requirements in Section 8-19-405 are satisfied. A person shall be limited to 378. 5 liters (100 gal) total coating per year under this exemption.

### 200 DEFINITIONS

### 201 Air-Dried Coatings

Any coating which is not heated above 90oC (194oF) for the purpose of curing or drying.

### 202 Baked Coatings

Any coating which is cured or dried in an oven where the oven air temperature exceeds 90oC (194oF).

### 203 Deleted June 7, 1989

### 204 Miscellaneous Metal Part or Product

Any metal part or product, except for those specified in Section 8-19-113.

### 206 Deleted June 7, 1989

### 207 Touch-up

**History**

- Amended 12/19/84; 12/18/85; 10/16/02

That portion of the surface prepar ation and coating operation which is incidental to the main coating process but necessary to cover minor imperfections or mechanical damage incurred prior to intended use.

### 208 Transfer Efficiency

The ratio of the amount of c oating solids adhering to the object being coated to the total amount of coati ng solids used in the application process, expressed as a percentage.

### 209 Camouflage Coating

**History**

- Amended December 2, 1981

A coating applied on military equipment to conceal such equipment from detection.

### 210 Pretreatment Wash Primer

**History**

- Adopted 12/19/84; Amended 6/7/89

Any coating which contains a minimum of 0.5% acid by weight, is necessary to provide surf ace etching and is applied directly to bare metal surfaces to provide corrosion resistance and adhesion.

### 211 Powder Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 19, 1984

Any coating applied as a dry (w ithout solvent or other carrier), finely divided solid which, when melted and fused, adheres to the substrate as a paint film.

### 212 Volatile Organic Compound

**History**

- Adopted 12/19/84; Amended 6/7/89; 2/3/93; 12/20/95; 10/16/02

Any organic compound (excluding methane, carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, carbonic acid, metallic carbides or carbonates and ammonium carbonate) which would be emi tted during use, application, curing or drying of a solvent or surface coating. 212.1 For purposes of calculating VOC content of a coating, any water or any of the following non-precursor organic compounds:

acetone methyl acetate parachlorobenzotrifluoride (PCBTF) cyclic, branched or linear, complete ly methylated siloxanes (VMS)

shall not be considered to be part of the coating.

#### 212.2

For the purposes of calculating the VOC content of a surface preparation or cleaning solvent, any water or the non-precursor organic compounds listed in 8-19-7 subsection 8-19-212.1, above, shall be considered part of the volume of solvent but shall not be considered part of the VOC content of the solvent.

### 213 Solid Film Lubricant

**History**

- Adopted 12/19/84; Amended 2/3/93

A very thin coating consis ting of an organic binder system containing as its chief pigment material one or more of molybdenum disulfide, graphite, polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) or ot her solids that act as a dry lubricant between meeting surfaces.

### 214 Adhesive

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which is applied for the purpose of bonding surfaces together.

### 215 Motor Vehicle

**History**

- Adopted January 7, 1987

A vehicle which is self-prope lled and is a device by which any person or property may be propelled, mov ed or drawn upon a highway, excepting a device moved by human power or used excl usively upon stationary rails or tracks.

### 216 Solar Absorbant Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which has as its primary purpose the absorption of solar radiation.

### 217 Heat -Resistant Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which during normal use must withstand temperatures of at least 204ºC (400ºF).

### 218 High -Gloss Coating

**History**

- Adopted12/18/85; Amended 6/7/89; 2/3/93

Any coating which achieves at least 85 percent reflectance on a 60º meter when tested by ASTM Method D-523-1989.

### 219 Extreme Performance Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which during intended use is exposed to one or more of the following conditions: 219.1 Repeated heavy abrasion, including mechanical wear and repeated scrubbing with industrial grade solvent s, detergents, cleaners or abrasive scouring agents

#### 219.2

Repeated exposure to temperatures in excess of 121ºC (250ºF), or below 0ºC (32ºF) or high vacuum conditions

#### 219.3

Chronic exposure to corrosive, caustic or acidic agents, chemicals, chemical fumes, chemical mixtures or solution.

### 220 Cathode Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which has as it s primary purpose the coating of electrical cathodes.

### 221 Chemical Milling Maskant Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which is applied to a component to protect areas when performing chem ical milling, anodizing, aging, bonding, plating, etching, and/or other chemic al surface operations on the component.

### 222 Non-Skid Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which has as it s primary purpose the creation of traction to prevent slippage.

### 223 Silicone Release Coating

**History**

- Adopted December 18, 1985

Any coating which contains silicone resin and has as its primary function the release of food products from metal surfaces such as baking pans.

### 224 Metallic Topcoat

**History**

- Adopted 12/18/85; Amended 1/7/87; 2/3/93

Any coating which contains more than 5 g/l (.042 lb/gal) of metal particles as identified on a technical or ma terial safety data sheet, as applied, where such metal particles are visible in the dried film.

### 225 High Performance Architectural Coating

**History**

- Adopted April 1, 1987

Any coating applied to architectural subsections and which is required to meet the specifications of Architectural Aluminum Manufacturer's Associati on's publication number AAMA 605.2-1980.

### 228 High-Temperature Coating

**History**

- Adopted 6/7/89; Amended 2/3/93

Any coating applied to a substrate which during normal use must withstand temperat ures of at least 538ºC (1000ºF).

### 229 Electrostatic Spray

Equipment used to apply coating by charging atomized particles that are deposited by electrosta tic attraction. (Adopted February 3, 1993)

### 230 High-Volume, Low-Pressure (HVLP) Spray

**History**

- Adopted 2/3/93; Amended 10/16/02

Equipment used to apply coating by means of a gun which is designed to be operated and which is operated between 0.1 and 10.0 psig air atomizing pressure measur ed dynamically at the center of the air cap and at the air horns.

### 231 Detailing Gun

**History**

- Adopted February 3, 1993

Small air-spray equipment, includi ng air brushes, that operate at no greater than 5 cfm air flow and no greater than 50 psig air pressure.

### 232 Cutback Asphalt Coating

**History**

- Adopted February 3, 1993

A protective coating c onsisting of a liquified asphalt blend of asphaltic cement (semi-solid resi due from the distillation of crude oil) and volatile petroleum distillates which is applied to large metal pipes to prevent corrosion.

### 233 Smooth Finish

**History**

- Adopted February 3, 1993

An industrial class 'A' surface coat ing finish that is characterized by a homogeneous, uninterrupted surface film wit hout any imperfections such as orange peel. This type of finish is not intended to have a subsequent textured finish coat applied.

### 234 Two-Tone Finish

**History**

- Adopted February 3, 1993

A surface coating finish where one color coating is applied adjacent to a previously coated substrate of a different color, without the use of a maskant. The interface between the two coati ngs is a clear division of colors without any overlap.

### 235 Approved Emission Control System

**History**

- Adopted February 3, 1993

A system for reducing emissions to the atmosphere, consisting of an abatement device and a collection system, which achieves the abatement efficiency specified in the applicable standards at all times during the operation and meets the require ments of Regulation 2, Rule 1.

### 236 Textured Finish Coat

**History**

- Adopted February 3, 1993

Any non-smooth, patterned surf ace that is intentionally produced and applied as a final coat by sprayi ng drops of coating over a previously applied base coating.

### 237 Mold Release Coating

**History**

- Adopted February 3, 1993

A temporary protective coating with a solids content of less than 120 grams solids per liter (1 pound solids per gallon) that reduces or prevents adhesion between the mold surfac e and the surface being molded.

### 238 Key System Operating Parameter

**History**

- Adopted 6/15/94; Amended 10/16/02

An emission control system operating parameter, such as temperature, flow rate or pressure, that ensures operation of the abatement equipment within manufacturer s pecifications and compliance with the standards in Sections 8-19-302, 312, 313, 320 and/or 321.

### 239 Surface Preparation

**History**

- Adopted October 16, 2002

The cleaning of metal parts and products prior to coating, further treatment, sale, or intended use. Surface preparation of metal parts subject to and in compliance with Regulation 8, Ru le 16: Solvent Cleaning Operations, is not subject to the surface preparat ion standards in this Rule.

### 240 Electrical and Electronic Components

**History**

- Adopted October 16, 2002

Components and assemblies of components that generate, convert, transmit, or modify electrical energy. Electrical and electronic components include, but are not limited to, wires, windings, stators, rotors, magnets, contacts, relays, printed circuit boards, printed wire assemblies, wiring boards, integrated circuits, resistors, capacitors and transistors. Cabinets in which electrical and electronic components are housed are not considered electrical and electronic components.

### 241 Medical Device

**History**

- Adopted October 16, 2002

An instrument, apparatus, implement, machine, contrivance, implant, in vitro reagent or other sim ilar article, including any component or accessory that is (i) intended for use in t he diagnosis of disease or other conditions, or in the cure, mitigation, treatment, or pr evention of diseases, or (ii) is intended to 8-19-9 affect the structure or any function of t he body, or (iii) is defined in the National Formulary or the United States Phar macopoeia or any supplement to it.
