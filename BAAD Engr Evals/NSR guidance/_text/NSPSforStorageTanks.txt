Policy: NSPS Requirements for Slotted Gauge Wells
and Automatic Bleeder Vents on New External Floating
Roof Tanks

Slotted Gauge    In order to achieve no visible gap with a slotted gauge well:
Well Policy
                 • The float in the gauge well must extend from the liquid surface to
                   the gauge well cover.
                 • The float must also have a flexible wipe-type seal.


Automatic        Any pressure/vacuum (P/V) valve used as a bleeder vent must have its
Bleeder Valve    set pressure such that it will not relieve under normal operations.
Policy


Rationale        These NSPS requirements are more stringent than Regulation 8-5:

                 Regulation 8-5 allows a ½” gap but NSPS Subpart Ka and Kb require
                 “no visible gap” (Section 60.112a(a)(1)(iii) and 60.112b(a)(2)(ii)).

                 Regulation 8-5 allows P/V valves to relieve at 0.5 psia or 10% of
                 maximum allowable working pressure. Subpart Ka and Kb do not
                 allow bleeder vents to relieve except when the roof is being floated off
                 or is being landed on the roof leg supports. (Section 60.112a(a)(1)(iii)
                 and 60.112b(a)(2)(ii)).
